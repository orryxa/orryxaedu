Q.2 Explain the following terms/concepts
1) E-business
Meaning: E-business (Electronic Business) is conducting business processes using internet, intranets, extranets. Coined by IBM in 1997. It includes buying/selling, customer service, supply chain, and technical support. Scope: B2B, B2C, C2C, C2B, B2A, C2A. Benefits: 24×7 access, global reach, lower cost, ease of formation.
2) B2B (Business to Business)
Both buyer and seller are business entities. Examples: Manufacturer supplying to wholesaler, company buying raw materials from another company. B2B transactions form the bulk of e-business value.
3) B2C (Business to Consumer)
Transactions between business firms and end consumers. Examples: Flipkart, Amazon where customers buy products directly. Features: online catalogs, shopping cart, home delivery.
4) C2C (Consumer to Consumer)
Transactions between consumers through a third-party platform. Example: eBay, OLX where one consumer sells to another. Platform acts as an intermediary.
5) Outsourcing
Contracting non-core business functions (sanitation, security, canteen, IT) to specialized agencies. Benefits: cost reduction, access to expertise, focus on core activities. Examples: Call centers, payroll processing.
6) BPO (Business Process Outsourcing)
Outsourcing of peripheral activities to minimize cost and increase efficiency. Types: Offshore (outside country), Onshore (same country), Nearshore (neighboring country). Examples: Customer support, data entry, telemarketing.
7) KPO (Knowledge Process Outsourcing)
Subset of BPO involving high-level knowledge-based tasks. Requires specialized skills: research, analytics, financial consulting, market research. Higher value addition than BPO.
8) LPO (Legal Process Outsourcing)
Outsourcing legal work to external firms: document review, contract drafting, patent filing, legal research, litigation support. Benefits: cost reduction, access to expertise, flexible staffing.

Q.5 Answer in brief (Detailed Explanations)
1) What is outsourcing? Illustrate with suitable example.
Outsourcing: Contracting business functions to specialized agencies. Companies outsource non-core activities to reduce cost and focus on core competencies.
Example: A software company outsources its canteen services, security, and housekeeping to specialized agencies. Similarly, a bank outsources its customer support call center to a BPO firm.
2) What is BPO? Explain in detail.
BPO (Business Process Outsourcing): Contracting of non-primary business operations to third-party providers. Types: Offshore (outside country), Onshore (same country), Nearshore (neighboring country). Examples: Customer support, telemarketing, data entry, payroll processing. Advantages: Cost reduction, productivity improvement, skilled manpower access. Disadvantages: Communication problems, time zone differences, loss of control.
3) What is KPO? Explain in detail.
KPO (Knowledge Process Outsourcing): Subset of BPO involving high-level, knowledge-based tasks. Requires specialized skills and advanced qualifications. Examples: Market research, financial analysis, data mining, pharmaceutical research, legal research, engineering design. Features: Higher value addition, requires analytical skills, professional expertise (MBA, CA, PhD).
4) What is LPO? Explain in detail.
LPO (Legal Process Outsourcing): Outsourcing legal work to external specialized firms. Services include: Document review, contract drafting, patent filing, legal research, litigation support, intellectual property management. Advantages: Cost reduction (up to 50-60%), access to expertise, flexible staffing, 24×7 operations. Challenges: Confidentiality concerns, cultural barriers, geographical distance.
Q.6 Justify the following statements
1) It is easy to set up e-business as compared to traditional business.
Justification: Traditional business requires physical shop, godown, licenses, staff, and huge capital. E-business can be started with a website, minimal investment, no physical store, and less paperwork. Registration is simple and can be done online. Hence, e-business is easier to set up.
2) E-business allows user to work across the globe in any field.
Justification: E-business uses the internet which has no geographical boundaries. A seller can reach customers worldwide, and a buyer can purchase from any country. It operates 24×7, allowing global access to markets, suppliers, and customers. Examples: Amazon, Alibaba enable cross-border trade.
3) Online transaction is done with the help of the internet.
Justification: Online transaction requires internet connection to access websites, place orders, and make payments. Without internet, steps like registration, adding to cart, payment via net banking/credit card cannot be completed. Hence, internet is the backbone of online transactions.

2) What are the types of e-business? Explain.
Six types of E-business models:
• B2B (Business to Business): Both parties are businesses (manufacturer to wholesaler)
• B2C (Business to Consumer): Business to end consumer (Flipkart, Amazon)
• C2B (Consumer to Business): Consumer requests service from business (pest control, taxi)
• C2C (Consumer to Consumer): Consumers selling to consumers (eBay, OLX)
• B2A (Business to Administration): Business to government (tax payment, permits)
• C2A (Consumer to Administration): Individual to government (passport, Aadhaar)

Q.8 Answer the following (Long Answer - Detailed Explanations)
1) Explain the steps involved in online transaction.
Online Transaction Procedure (3 main steps):

Step 1: Registration — First step. Customer registers on website by filling form (name, email, address, password). Account created with shopping cart to track selected items.

Step 2: Placing an Order — Customer browses products, selects items, adds to shopping cart. Cart shows items, quantity, price. Customer proceeds to checkout.

Step 3: Payment Mechanism — Last step. Customer chooses payment option:
a) Cash on Delivery: Pay at doorstep on delivery
b) Cheque: Send cheque to seller; goods after realization
c) Net Banking Transfer: Electronic transfer from buyer to seller account
d) Credit/Debit Card: Payment via plastic money; amount deducted instantly
e) Digital Cash: Electronic currency for online transactions

After payment, seller dispatches goods. Information plays vital role in all stages.
