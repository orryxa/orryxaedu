Q.1 (H) Answer in one sentence
1) What do you mean by 'Local Market'?
Local market refers to the market for commodities which are sold within local geographical limits of a region.
2) What do you mean by 'Regulated Market'?
Regulated market refers to the markets regulated by statutory provisions of the country, e.g., Stock Exchanges, Commodity Exchanges.

Market is the place where two or more parties (buyers and sellers) are involved in buying and selling with exchange of money.
4) What do you mean by standardization?
Standardization means to determine standards related to process, size, quality, design, weight, colour of the product.
5) What do you mean by Branding?
Branding is a process of giving special identity to a product through unique brand name to differentiate from competitors.

Q.2 Explain the following terms/concepts
1) Market
Market: Derived from Latin word 'mercatus' meaning 'to trade'. Market is the place where buyers and sellers come together to exchange goods/services for money. Modern concept includes digital markets (E-commerce).
2) Place Concept of Market
Traditional concept where market is understood as a physical place where buying and selling occurs (e.g., market yard, shopping mall). In olden days, place played important role.
3) Commodity Concept of Market
Emphasis on 'buying and selling of goods/services' rather than place. Process of exchange and commodity exchanged plays important role.
4) Digital Concept of Market
Uses Information Technology for buying/selling. E-commerce portals, mobile apps make buying easy. Communication media like internet, mobile enable direct contact between customer and seller.
5) Product
Product refers to goods or services offered to customers for sale, capable of satisfying customer needs. Can be tangible (goods) or intangible (services).
6) Price
Price is the amount a customer pays for product. It creates demand. Too high price affects demand; too low affects profitability.
7) Promotion
Promotion is marketing communication tool to publicise product to customers, convey features, induce buying. Includes advertising, direct marketing, sales promotion, personal selling.
8) Marketing Mix
Combination of different marketing variables blended to achieve desired result from target market. 4Ps (Product, Price, Place, Promotion) for goods; 7Ps (+People, Process, Physical Evidence) for services.
9) Packaging
Packaging means designing package for product to avoid breakage, damage, destruction. Includes bottles, containers, plastic bags, tin, wooden boxes, bubble bags.
10) Labeling
Label is a slip on product providing information about product and producer. Includes price, manufacturer, expiry date, batch number, user manual, safety instructions.

Q.4 Answer in brief
1) Explain in detail the significance of marketing to the society.
6 Importance to Society:
1. Increase in Standard of Living: Provides quality goods at cheaper prices.
2. Provides Employment: Covers buying, selling, finance, transport, warehousing.
3. Decreases Distribution Costs: Effective channel of distribution reduces costs.
4. Consumer Awareness: Informs about new products, features, usefulness.
5. Increase in National Income: Economic development, industrialization, more jobs.
6. Managing Consumer Expectations: Marketing research helps understand consumer needs.
2) Explain 4Ps of product marketing mix.
4Ps by E. Jerome McCarthy (1960):
• Product: Goods/services offered to satisfy customer needs. Includes design, quality, features, branding, packaging.
• Price: Amount customer pays. Determined by cost, demand, competition, desired profit.
• Place (Distribution): Making product available at right place. Includes channels, logistics, warehousing, transport.
• Promotion: Communicating product to customers. Includes advertising, sales promotion, personal selling, publicity.
3) Explain 3Ps in marketing of services.
3 Additional Ps by Booms & Bitner (1981) for Service Marketing:
• People: All human beings playing active role in delivering service. Employees, customers, recruit right people, train them.
• Process: Steps involved in delivering products/services to customers. Ensures same standard, saves time and money.
• Physical Evidence: Environment where customer-firm interaction occurs. Location, layout, interior design, packaging, staff dress, waiting area.
4) Explain types of market on the basis of area covered.
On Basis of Area Covered:
• Local Market: Within local geographical limits (e.g., village market).
• National Market: Within the country (e.g., domestic trade).
• International Market: Across countries (export/import).
5) Explain types of market on the basis of time.
On Basis of Time:
• Very Short Period Market: Few hours or a day; perishable goods (vegetables, fruits, milk).
• Short Period Market: Weekly markets, festival markets; perishable/semi-durable goods.
• Long Period Market: Long existence; durable, non-perishable goods.

Q.5 Justify the following statements
1) Marketing is significant to the consumers.
Justification: Marketing promotes product awareness, provides quality products, offers variety, helps in selection, ensures consumer satisfaction, and regular supply of goods. It educates consumers about new products and their usefulness, helping them make informed buying decisions.
2) Promotion plays an important role in marketing.
Justification: Promotion creates brand recognition and sales. It conveys product features to potential buyers, inducing them to purchase. Tools like advertising, direct marketing, sales promotion, personal selling help increase brand awareness. Without promotion, customers may never know about product's existence.
3) Market can be classified on the basis of competition.
Justification: On competition basis: Perfect Market (large number buyers/sellers, homogeneous products, single price) and Imperfect Market (market imperfections). Imperfect markets further divided into Monopoly (single seller), Duopoly (two sellers), Oligopoly (few sellers), Monopsony (single buyer).
4) Marketing helps in increasing consumer awareness.
Justification: Marketing informs and educates consumers about new products/services, their features, uses, prices, availability. Through advertisements, brochures, social media, consumers get relevant information to make purchase decisions. Campaigns like 'Jago Grahak Jago' increase awareness.
5) There is a need of branding to get recognition among the consumers.
Justification: Branding gives special identity to product through unique brand name, differentiating from competitors. Registered brands (trademarks) cannot be copied. Branding helps get recognition, builds customer loyalty, increases brand awareness, and helps in getting new business.
Q.6 Attempt the following
1) Give classification of market in detail on the basis of 'Volume of Transaction'.
On Basis of Volume of Transaction:
• Wholesale Market: Buying and selling in large quantities at cheaper prices. Goods sold to retailers. Sellers are wholesalers, buyers are retailers.
• Retail Market: Retailer sells goods directly to consumers in small quantities. Final link in distribution channel.
2) Give classification of market on the basis of 'Time'.
On Basis of Time:
• Very Short Period Market: Few hours/day; perishable goods (vegetables, fruits, milk).
• Short Period Market: Weekly/festival markets; perishable/semi-durable goods.
• Long Period Market: Long existence; durable, non-perishable goods.
3) Explain packaging and labelling.
Packaging: Designing package for product to avoid breakage, damage, destruction. Includes bottles, containers, plastic bags, tin, wooden boxes, bubble bags. Creates first impression.
Labelling: Label is slip on product providing information about product and producer. Includes price, manufacturer, date of manufacture, expiry date, batch number, user manual, safety instructions, ISI/AGMARK marks.
4) Explain standardisation and grading.
Standardisation: Determining standards for process, size, quality, design, weight, colour. Ensures uniformity, achieves customer loyalty.
Grading: Classification of products according to similar characteristics/quality (size, shape, quality). Generally done for agricultural products like wheat, rice, potatoes.
5) Explain importance of marketing to the firm.
6 Importance to Firm:
1. Increases Awareness about products and company.
2. Increases Sales through successful campaigns.
3. Creates Trust among customers, builds loyalty.
4. Basis for Making Decisions (what, how, when to produce).
5. Source of New Ideas through feedback and research.
6. Tackling the Competition by creating brand image.
Q.7 & Q.8 Detailed Answers
Q.7 (1) Define Marketing and explain in detail the concepts of marketing.
Definition (Philip Kotler): "Marketing is a societal process by which individuals and groups obtain what they need and want through creating, offering and freely exchanging products and services of value with others."
6 Concepts of Market:
1. Place Concept: Physical place where buying/selling occurs.
2. Commodity Concept: Emphasis on buying/selling of goods/services.
3. Exchange Concept: Voluntary exchange with free consent, mutual trust.
4. Area Concept: Free association between buyers/sellers to fix prices.
5. Demand/Customer Concept: Aggregate demand by potential buyers.
6. Space/Digital Concept: Using IT for buying/selling (E-commerce).
Q.7 (2) Explain different types of market in detail.
7 Basis of Market Classification:
1. Area Covered: Local, National, International.
2. Time: Very Short Period, Short Period, Long Period.
3. Volume of Transaction: Wholesale, Retail.
4. Importance: Primary (agricultural), Secondary (semi-processed), Terminal (ultimate consumers).
5. Nature of Goods: Commodity Market, Capital Market.
6. Regulation: Regulated (Stock Exchange), Unregulated (free market).
7. Competition: Perfect Market, Imperfect Market (Monopoly, Duopoly, Oligopoly, Monopsony).
Q.7 (3) Explain in detail 7Ps of Marketing.
7Ps of Marketing Mix:
Product: Goods/services satisfying customer needs.
Price: Amount customer pays.
Place: Distribution channels, availability.
Promotion: Advertising, sales promotion, personal selling.
People: Employees delivering service.
Process: Steps in delivering service.
Physical Evidence: Environment where interaction occurs.

Q.7 (5) Explain importance of marketing to the society and consumers.
To Society: Increases standard of living, provides employment, decreases distribution costs, consumer awareness, increases national income, manages consumer expectations.
To Consumers: Promotes product awareness, provides quality products, provides variety, helps in selection, ensures consumer satisfaction, regular supply of goods.
