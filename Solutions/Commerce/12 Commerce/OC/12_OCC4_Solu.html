Q.2 Explain the following terms/concepts
1) Transport
Transport is the movement of people, animals and goods from one location to another. Modes include road, rail, air, water, pipeline, ropeway, monorail. It creates place utility and is essential for trade.
2) Communication
Communication is the art of exchanging ideas, facts, information using postal (mail, eMO, iMO, ePost) or modern means (courier, internet, email). It facilitates business transactions and decision-making.
3) Banking
Banking refers to accepting deposits from public for lending/investment, repayable on demand, and providing other financial services like fund transfer, locker facility, bill payment, etc.
4) Insurance
Insurance is a contract where insurer compensates insured against specified losses in exchange for premium. It is a risk management tool based on principles like utmost good faith, insurable interest, indemnity, etc.
5) Warehousing
Warehousing is storage of goods from production till consumption, creating time utility and price stability. Functions include storage, risk bearing, financing, grading, packing, and processing.

i) Life insurance for wife & children? Yes — anyone with insurable interest can take.

Q.5 Answer in Brief (Detailed Explanations)
1) State four types of deposits.
Fixed Deposit (FD): Fixed amount for fixed period, higher interest, FDR issued.
Recurring Deposit (RD): Monthly fixed deposit, lump sum on maturity.
Saving Account: For individuals, low interest, passbook facility.
Current Account: For businessmen, no interest, overdraft facility.
2) State four modes of transport.
Road Transport: Door-to-door, flexible, short distance.
Rail Transport: Bulk goods, long distance, cost-effective.
Air Transport: Fastest, valuable/perishable goods, expensive.
Water Transport: Cheapest for bulky goods, international trade.
3) State four life insurance policies.
Whole Life: Insured for whole life; amount on death.
Endowment: Sum assured + bonus on maturity or death.
Term Insurance: Pure protection, lowest premium.
Money-back: Periodic survival benefits + full sum assured.
4) State any four features of business services.
Intangibility: Cannot be seen/touched.
Inseparability: Service & provider cannot be separated.
Inconsistency: Quality varies each time.
Perishability: Cannot be stored; lost if not used.
5) State money remittance services of postal department.
eMO (Electronic Money Order): Traditional money order electronically.
iMO (Instant Money Order): Web-based instant transfer ₹1000-50,000.
International Money Transfer: Inward remittances via Western Union.
Q.6 Justify the following statements
1) Air transport is fastest mode of transport.
True — Because it travels at very high speed (900+ km/h), covers long distances in hours, suitable for emergency & perishable goods.
2) Communication is essential for growth of business.
True — Without communication, orders, payments, market information cannot flow; modern business relies on instant communication.
3) Principle of subrogation is applicable to all contracts of indemnity.
True — After compensation, insurer gets rights over damaged property; prevents insured from profiting twice.
4) Warehousing is important.
True — Creates time utility, stabilizes prices, provides storage, enables financing, reduces risk.
5) Cash can be withdrawn from ATM at any time.
True — ATMs operate 24×7, provide round-the-clock cash withdrawal and other banking services.
Q.7 Attempt the following (Detailed Explanations)
1) Explain money remittance services of post department.
India Post offers three main money remittance services: eMO (Electronic Money Order — traditional money order delivered at doorstep), iMO (Instant Money Order — web-based instant transfer ₹1,000-50,000), and International Money Transfer (inward remittances from 195+ countries via Western Union).
2) Explain marine insurance policies.
Types: Voyage (specific voyage), Time (fixed period), Mixed (voyage+time), Valued (agreed value), Blanket (max limit), Port risk (vessel at port), Fleet (several ships of same owner), Block (cargo in all transport modes).
3) Explain types of warehouses.
Types: Private (own use), Public (general public, fee), Bonded (imported goods before duty), Duty paid (after duty), Government (CWC, FCI), Co-operative (societies), Cold storage (perishable goods).
4) Explain utility functions of banks.
Utility functions include: Issue of drafts/cheques, locker facility, project reports, gift cheques & gold coins, underwriting services, gold-related services (buying/selling, gold funds, ETFs).
5) Explain modes of traditional communication.
Traditional postal modes: Inland Letter (confidential), Envelope (enclosures), Parcel (insured), Book Post (printed matter), Registered Post (proof of delivery), Speed Post (fast delivery).
6) Explain disadvantages of air transport.
Disadvantages: High cost, weather dependent, limited capacity, no door-to-door service, high infrastructure investment, international restrictions, not suitable for short distances.
7) Describe the role of transport.
Role: Mobility of goods & people, creates place utility, expands market, supports industrialization, employment generation, promotes international trade, emergency services during calamities.
8) What are the functions of warehouses?
Functions: Storage, price stabilization, risk bearing, financing, grading & packing, transportation, time & place utility, processing (ripening, polishing, blending).
Q.8 Answer the following (Long Answer Type - Detailed Explanations)
1) What is insurance? Explain principles of insurance.
Insurance: Contract where insurer compensates insured against losses for premium.
7 Principles:
• Utmost Good Faith — disclose all material facts
• Insurable Interest — financial interest in subject matter
• Indemnity — compensate actual loss (not for life insurance)
• Subrogation — rights transfer to insurer after compensation
• Contribution — proportionate payment by multiple insurers
• Mitigation of Loss — insured must minimize loss
• Causa-Proxima — nearest cause determines liability.
2) Define bank. Explain different types of banks.
Definition: Bank accepts deposits from public for lending/investment, repayable on demand (Banking Regulation Act, 1949).
Types: Central (RBI), Commercial (SBI, HDFC), Co-operative (primary/district/state), Development (IFCI, SIDBI, EXIM), Exchange (forex), RRB (rural areas), Savings (Postal), Investment (M&A), Specialised (NABARD), Small Finance & Payment Banks.
3) What is warehouse? Explain its different functions.
Warehouse: Establishment for storage/accumulation of goods from production to consumption.
Functions: Storage, price stabilization, risk bearing, financing (loans against warehouse receipts), grading & packing, transportation, time & place utility, processing (ripening fruits, polishing paddy, blending tea).
4) What is Services? Explain in detail different business services.
Services: Intangible acts that do not result in ownership (Philip Kotler).
Business Services:
• Banking — deposits, loans, e-banking (ATM, RTGS, NEFT, IMPS)
• Insurance — risk protection, 7 principles, life/marine/fire
• Transport — moves goods/people; road, rail, air, water, pipeline
• Warehousing — storage, price stability, financing
• Communication — postal (mail, eMO, iMO) & modern (courier, internet, email).
5) What is communication? Explain in detail various types of communication.
Communication: Art of exchanging ideas/information using medium.
Postal Services: Mail (inland, envelope, parcel), Specialised (Business Post, Logistics, Bill Mail), Money Remittance (eMO, iMO, International), Retail (e-Post).
Modern Means: Courier (speed, tracking), Internet (global network, WWW), Email (electronic mail, store-and-forward).
6) What is road transport? Explain its advantages and disadvantages.
Road Transport: Movement on land using vehicles (buses, trucks, cars). India has village roads, highways; MoRTH oversees development.
Advantages: Door-to-door, flexible, low cost for short distance, suitable for perishable goods, feeder to other modes.
Disadvantages: Limited capacity, weather affected, accidents, pollution, not economical for long distance.
