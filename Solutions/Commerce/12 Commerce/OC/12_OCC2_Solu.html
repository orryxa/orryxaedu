📘 Chapter Summary: Functions of Management

🔹 Introduction: Management is a set of principles relating to various functions like planning, organizing, staffing, directing, coordinating & controlling. According to Henry Fayol: "To manage is to forecast and to plan, to organize, to command and to control."

Planning

Deciding in advance what, how, when & who

Organising

Grouping & assigning activities

Staffing

Recruit, select, train & develop

Directing

Guide, inspire & supervise

Coordinating

Harmonize group efforts

Controlling

Compare actual vs standard
📌 Planning Importance: Sets objectives, Provides path, Improves performance, Minimizes risk, Optimum utilization, Decision making, Sets standards for control, Facilitates coordination.

🔹 Organising: Process of identifying & bringing together resources (men, money, material, machine, method). Brings specialization, defines jobs, clarifies authority-responsibility, establishes coordination.

🔹 Staffing: Attracting, recruiting, selecting, placing, appraising, remunerating, developing & retaining best workforce. "Right person at right job with right pay"

🔹 Directing (Soul of Management): Instructing, guiding, communicating, inspiring, motivating & supervising. Initiates action, integrates efforts, means of motivation, creates team spirit.

🔹 Coordinating (Plus-value of the group - Mary Parker Follett): Integration & synchronization of group efforts. Encourages team spirit, gives proper direction, facilitates motivation, optimum utilization.

🔹 Controlling: Comparing actual performance with predetermined standards, measuring deviation, identifying causes & suggesting corrective measures. Koontz & O'Donnell: "Managerial control implies measurement of accomplishment against standard."

⭐ Management Cycle: Planning → Organising → Staffing → Directing → Coordinating → Controlling → (leads to next plan)

H. Answer in one sentence

1) What is management?
Management is a set of principles relating to functions like planning, organizing, staffing, directing, coordinating and controlling to achieve organizational goals.
2) What is planning?
Planning is deciding in advance what to do, how to do it, when to do it and who is to do it.
3) What is staffing?
Staffing is the process of attracting, recruiting, selecting, placing, appraising, remunerating, developing and retaining the best workforce.
4) What is directing?
Directing is the process of instructing, guiding, inspiring, motivating and supervising employees to achieve organizational goals.
5) What is controlling?
Controlling is comparing actual performance with predetermined standards and taking corrective action for deviations.

Importance of Management Functions (Key Points)

📌 Planning Importance
Sets objectives, Provides path, Improves performance, Minimizes risk, Optimum utilization, Helps decision making, Sets standards, Facilitates coordination
📌 Organising Importance
Facilitates administration, Brings specialization, Defines jobs, Clarifies authority-responsibility, Establishes coordination, Helps growth, Creates security, Scope for innovation
📌 Staffing Importance
Effective managerial function, Utilizes human resources, Builds cordial relationship, Human resource development, Effective use of technology, Improves efficiency, Long term effect, Job satisfaction
📌 Directing Importance
Initiates action, Integrates efforts, Means of motivation, Provides stability, Coping with changes, Effective resource utilization, Creates team spirit
📌 Coordinating Importance
Encourages team spirit, Gives proper direction, Facilitates motivation, Optimum utilization, Achieves objectives, Improves relations, Higher efficiency, Builds goodwill
📌 Controlling Importance
Fulfills goals, Efficient resource utilization, Accuracy of standards, Motivates employees, Ensures order & discipline, Facilitates coordination, Builds corporate image
