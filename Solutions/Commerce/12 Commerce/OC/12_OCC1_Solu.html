📘 Chapter Summary: Principles of Management

🔹 Meaning of Principles: Fundamental truths serving as foundation for belief/behavior. Management principles are universal, flexible guidelines formed through practice & experiments.

📌 Nature of Principles: Universal application, General guidelines, Formed by practice, Flexibility, Behavioral, Cause-effect relationship, Equal importance.

🔹 Significance: Provides insight to managers, Efficient resource utilization, Scientific decisions, Social responsibility understanding, Encourages R&D, Coordination & control, Develops objective approach.

🔹 Henry Fayol (Father of Modern Management): 14 Principles: Division of Work, Authority & Responsibility, Discipline, Unity of Command, Unity of Direction, Subordination of Individual Interest, Centralization, Remuneration, Scalar Chain, Order, Equity, Stability of Tenure, Initiative, Esprit de corps (Teamwork).

🔹 F.W. Taylor (Father of Scientific Management): Principles: Science not Rule of Thumb, Harmony not Discord, Mental Revolution, Cooperation not Individualism, Division of Responsibility, Development for greater efficiency.

⭐ Techniques of Scientific Management: Work Study (Time, Method, Motion, Fatigue), Standardization, Scientific Task Setting, Scientific Selection & Training, Functional Organization (8 foremen), Differential Piece-Rate Wage Plan.

💡 Famous Quote (C.K. Prahlad): "The essence of strategy lies in creating tomorrow's competitive advantages faster than competitors can mimic the ones you possess today."

F. Answer in one sentence

1) What is principle of unity of command?
Each member of organization should receive orders from only one superior to avoid confusion and conflict.
2) What is standardization of tools and equipments?
It means setting uniform tools, machines and working conditions to reduce waste, fatigue and improve quality.
3) What is differential wage rate?
Taylor's wage plan where efficient workers get higher rate and inefficient get lower rate to motivate productivity.
4) What is Subordination of individual interest into organizational interest?
Organizational interest should prevail over personal interest while making decisions.
5) What is the meaning of principle?
A fundamental truth or proposition serving as foundation for a system of belief or behavior.

Henri Fayol's 14 Principles of Management

#PrincipleExplanation
1Division of WorkWork divided into small tasks → specialization → efficiency.
2Authority & ResponsibilityRight to give orders comes with accountability.
3DisciplineRespect for rules & agreements; essential for smooth functioning.
4Unity of CommandEach employee receives orders from one superior only.
5Unity of DirectionOne head, one plan for each group of activities.
6Subordination of Individual InterestOrganizational interest > personal interest.
7CentralizationAppropriate concentration of authority depending on size.
8RemunerationFair wages based on skill, cost of living, profitability.
9Scalar ChainHierarchy from top to bottom; Gang Plank for direct communication.
10OrderRight place for everything and everyone.
11EquityKindness & justice; no discrimination.
12Stability of TenureJob security to build loyalty & efficiency.
13InitiativeEncourage employees to think & execute new ideas.
14Esprit de CorpsTeamwork & harmony → union is strength.

Fayol vs Taylor: Two Pillars of Management

BasisHenry Fayol (Father of Modern Management)F.W. Taylor (Father of Scientific Management)
FocusTop-level management, overall administrationShop-floor level, operational efficiency
ApproachGeneral theory of management (14 principles)Scientific methods & experiments
Key ConceptUnity of command, scalar chain, equityTime & motion study, differential piece wage
Principle emphasisUniversal applicability & flexibilityScience not rule of thumb, harmony, cooperation

Time, Method, Motion & Fatigue Study
Standardization
Tools, equipment & working conditions
Scientific Task Setting
Fair day's work & standard output
Scientific Selection & Training
Right person on right job + training
Functional Organization
8 foremen (4 planning + 4 implementation)
Differential Piece Rate
Higher wage for above-standard output
⭐ Taylor's 8 Foremen: Planning Level: Route Clerk, Instruction Clerk, Time & Cost Clerk, Discipline. Implementation: Gang Boss, Speed Boss, Repair Boss, Inspector.
