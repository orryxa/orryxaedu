Q.2 Explain the following terms/concepts
1) District Commission
District Commission: Consumer dispute redressal forum at district level established by State Government. Composition: President (District Judge) + 2+ members. Monetary Jurisdiction: Value ≤ ₹1 crore. Appeal: To State Commission within 45 days.
2) National Commission
National Commission: Apex consumer dispute redressal forum at national level established by Central Government. Composition: President (Supreme Court Judge) + 4+ members. Monetary Jurisdiction: Value > ₹10 crore. Appeal: To Supreme Court within 30 days.
3) State Commission
State Commission: Consumer dispute redressal forum at state level established by State Government. Composition: President (High Court Judge) + 4+ members. Monetary Jurisdiction: ₹1 crore < value ≤ ₹10 crore. Appeal: To National Commission within 30 days.
4) Lok Adalat
Lok Adalat (People's Court): Effective and economical system for quick redressal of public grievances. Disputes settled by compromise. Decisions get statutory recognition. Example: MSEDCL, MSRTC, Banks organize regular Lok Adalats.
5) Janhit Yachika (Public Interest Litigation)
PIL: Legal action initiated in court regarding matter of general public interest. Any person can approach court in interest of society. Provides legal remedy to unrepresented groups. Filed in High Court or Supreme Court.
Q.3 Study the case/situation and express your opinion
1) Mr. Ashok forced to buy bigger size shoes; shopkeeper refused to replace despite stock.
i) Right violated: Right to Choose
ii) Comment: Right to Choose gives consumer freedom to select article as per requirement, liking, purchasing capacity. Seller cannot compel consumer to buy particular product. Here salesman forced bigger size shoes, violating this right.
iii) Complaint filing: Mr. Ashok can file complaint in District Commission (value ₹700 < ₹1 crore).
2) Mrs. Meera bought washing machine worth ₹50,000 without cash memo; defect after 1.5 years; company refused repair.
i) Redressal machinery: District Commission (value ₹50,000 < ₹1 crore)
ii) Appeal if unsatisfied: State Commission
iii) Negligence: Mrs. Meera did not take cash memo/invoice and did not check guarantee/warrantee card properly. Cash memo is essential evidence for consumer complaint.

Q.5 Answer in brief
1) Explain any four needs of consumer protection.
Four needs of consumer protection:
1. Ignorance of consumers: Most consumers are ignorant about their rights, market conditions, product details.
2. Unorganized consumers: Consumers are widely scattered and not organized, while sellers are powerful.
3. Spurious goods: Increasing supply of duplicate products; ordinary consumer cannot distinguish genuine from imitation.
4. Misleading advertising: Businessmen give misleading information about quality, safety, utility; consumers are misled.
2) State any four rights of the consumer.
Four consumer rights:
1. Right to Safety: Protection against hazardous products (ISI, AGMARK, Hallmark).
2. Right to Information: Adequate information about price, manufacturer, expiry date.
3. Right to Choose: Freedom to select product as per requirement and capacity.
4. Right to be Heard: Opportunity to voice complaints and suggestions.
3) Explain any four responsibilities of the consumer.
Four consumer responsibilities:
1. Cautious consumer: Enquire about quality, quantity, price, utility before buying.
2. Quality conscious: Look for ISI, AGMARK, Hallmark, FPO, FSSAI marks.
3. Filing complaint: Approach concerned officer if goods defective; don't delay.
4. Demand invoice: Always ask for cash memo and guarantee/warrantee card.
4) State the role of NGO in consumer protection.
Role of NGOs in consumer protection:
1. Organize campaigns and programmes on consumer issues to create awareness.
2. Organize training programmes for consumers about rights and redressal modes.
3. Publish periodicals to enlighten consumers about developments.
4. Provide free legal advice to members and help take up grievances.
5. File Public Interest Litigation on important consumer issues.
Examples: CGSI, VOICE, CERC, Mumbai Grahak Panchayat, Grahak Shakti.

Q.6 Justify the following statements
1) The Consumer Protection Act was passed in the interest of consumers.
Justification: The Act provides three-tier quasi-judicial machinery (District, State, National Commissions) for speedy, simple, inexpensive redressal. It gives consumers rights like safety, information, choice, redressal. It covers unfair trade practices, defective goods, deficient services. Hence, Act is definitely in interest of consumers.
2) Consumers have many responsibilities.
Justification: Along with rights, consumers must be cautious, check quality marks (ISI/AGMARK), demand invoice, file complaints for defective products, avoid exaggerating advertisements, and support consumer organizations. Without responsibilities, rights cannot be effectively exercised.
3) Aim of consumer organization is to protect the rights of the consumer.
Justification: Consumer organizations (NGOs) organize awareness campaigns, provide legal advice, file PILs, publish periodicals, and help consumers file complaints. They educate consumers about their rights and redressal mechanisms. Their sole aim is consumer welfare and protection.

Q.8 (2) Explain the ways and means of Consumer Protection.
7 Ways & Means:
1. Lok Adalat: People's Court — quick compromise redressal.
2. Public Interest Litigation: Legal action for public interest.
3. Redressal Forums: District/State/National Commissions.
4. Awareness Programme: Govt journals, brochures, 15th March (World), 24th Dec (National).
5. Consumer Organizations: NGOs like CGSI, VOICE.
6. Consumer Welfare Fund: Financial assistance for consumer movement.
7. Legislative Measures: Sale of Goods Act 1930, BIS Act 1969, Food Safety Act 2006, CPA 2019.
Q.8 (3) Explain the three tier quasi judicial machinery under the Act.
Three-Tier Quasi-Judicial Machinery:
1. District Commission: At district level. President: District Judge. Monetary jurisdiction: upto ₹1 crore. Appeal to State Commission.
2. State Commission: At state level. President: High Court Judge. Monetary jurisdiction: ₹1 crore to ₹10 crore. Appeal to National Commission.
3. National Commission: At national level (apex). President: Supreme Court Judge. Monetary jurisdiction: exceeds ₹10 crore. Appeal to Supreme Court.
Penalty for non-compliance: Imprisonment 1 month to 3 years OR fine ₹25,000 to ₹1 lakh OR both.
