Q.2 Explain the following terms/concepts
1) Social responsibility
Meaning: Social responsibility of business implies the obligation of management to protect the interest of society. According to Keith Davis: "Social responsibility refers to obligations of businessman's decisions and actions beyond firm's direct economic interest." Business must take decisions that are desirable in terms of society's objectives and values.
2) Concept of Trusteeship
Meaning: Trusteeship is a socio-economic philosophy propounded by Mahatma Gandhi. It provides a means by which wealthy people would be trustees of trusts that look after welfare of people. Businessmen are considered trustees of society, managing resources for the benefit of all stakeholders, not just for personal profit.
3) Business ethics
Meaning: Business ethics is the study of business situations where issues of right and wrong are addressed. It is a code of conduct that businesses are expected to follow. Derived from Greek word 'Ethos' meaning character. It deals with concepts like right/wrong, good/bad, fair/unfair, just/unjust, legal/illegal in business actions.
4) Moral Values
Meaning: Moral values are principles that guide individuals to distinguish between right and wrong. Examples include honesty, integrity, fairness, hard work, cooperation, truthfulness, compassion. These values help in personal character development and ethical decision-making in business.
5) Social values
Meaning: Social values are general guidelines for social conduct. They form an important part of society's culture. Examples include fundamental rights, patriotism, respect for human dignity, rationality, sacrifice, equality, democracy, justice. They guide behaviour in social context.

Q.4 Answer in brief
1) Give any four types of social responsibilities towards consumers.
Four responsibilities towards consumers:
• Good Quality Products: Produce quality goods with ISO, BIS, AGMARK standards
• Fair Prices: Charge reasonable prices, print MRP on packets
• Customer's Safety: Ensure products do not adversely affect life/health
• Honest Advertising: Avoid false, misleading, vulgar advertisements
• After Sales Service: Provide maintenance during warranty period
• Attend Complaints: Quick redressal of consumer grievances
2) What is business ethics? What are elements of business ethics?
Business Ethics: Code of conduct that businesses are expected to follow, dealing with right/wrong, good/bad in business actions.
Elements/Features (8):
1. Code of conduct
2. Based on moral and social values
3. Protection to social groups
4. Provides basic framework
5. Voluntary (not enforced by law)
6. Requires education and guidance
7. Relative term (changes across countries)
8. Newer concept
3) State responsibilities of business towards government.
Responsibilities towards government:
1. Timely payment of taxes (income tax, sales tax, GST)
2. Observing rules and regulations (licenses, laws)
3. Earning foreign exchange through exports
4. Contributing to economic development
5. Implementing socio-economic policies
6. Providing suggestions to government
7. No favours (avoid bribery)
8. Contributing during emergencies/natural calamities

Q.5 Justify the following statements
1) Role of media has major influence on business organisation.
Justification: Media plays important role in public life. Due to internet, it reaches common man easily. Media is vibrant and active; it can influence masses in society. Media can raise voice against business malpractices, exploitation of consumers, unfair trade practices. Business cannot ignore social values because media exposure can damage reputation and brand image.
2) Business should allow workers participation in management.
Justification: Workers participation in management raises morale and gives them sense of belonging. It can be encouraged through workers committees, suggestion schemes, profit sharing. Participation leads to better understanding between management and workers, reduces conflicts, increases productivity, and ensures industrial peace. It makes workers feel valued and motivated.
3) Expectations of society towards business are changing.
Justification: In early days, business was viewed only as provider of goods/services. Today society expects business to be a responsible citizen contributing towards social welfare. Society expects environmental protection, fair trade practices, employee welfare, consumer protection, community development. Businesses must adapt to these changing expectations for long-term survival.
4) Business organisations should avoid environmental pollution and ecological imbalance.
Justification: Industrialisation causes air, water, sound pollution and electronic garbage. Environmental issues like loss of biodiversity, ozone depletion threaten human survival. Protection of environment benefits business through cost savings, better reputation, less government control. 4 R's (Reduce, Reuse, Recycle, Recover) should be followed for waste management.
5) All sorts of unfair practices related to stock exchange should be avoided.
Justification: Unfair practices like insider trading, providing wrong/secret information, artificially increasing/decreasing share prices lead to loss to common investors. Such practices destroy investor confidence, harm capital market growth, and are illegal. Fair practices ensure transparency, protect small investors, and maintain market integrity.
6) Business ethics contains moral and social values for doing business.
Justification: Business ethics includes self-control, consumer protection, service to society, fair treatment, not exploiting others. It contains principles for distinguishing right from wrong. Moral values (honesty, integrity) and social values (equality, justice) guide ethical business conduct. Without these values, business cannot achieve long-term success.
7) Business ethics can be considered as a tool for social development.
Justification: Business ethics ensures fair treatment to consumers, employees, investors, society. It prevents exploitation, encourages honest advertising, promotes environmental protection, ensures quality products, fair wages. By following ethics, businesses contribute to social welfare, economic development, and sustainable growth. Ethics thus becomes a powerful tool for social development.

Responsibilities towards investors (6 points):
1. Proper conduct of meetings (notices, information during crisis)
2. Return on investment (fair returns, safety, steady appreciation)
3. Handling grievances (effective methodology)
4. Maintain transparency in operations
5. Proper disclosure of information (regular reports, financial performance)
6. Maintain solvency and prestige (sound financial position, innovation, expansion)
4) Describe the features of business ethics.
Features of Business Ethics (8 points):
1. Code of conduct (what to do/what not to do)
2. Based on moral and social values (self-control, fair treatment)
3. Gives protection to social groups (consumers, employees, shareholders)
4. Provides basic framework (social, cultural, legal limits)
5. Voluntary (self-discipline, not enforced by law)
6. Requires education and guidance (motivation needed)
7. Relative term (changes across businesses and countries)
8. New concept (strictly followed in developed countries)
5) What is corporate social responsibility?
CSR (Corporate Social Responsibility): Self-regulating business model aiming to contribute to societal goals. Under Companies Act 2013 Section 135 (effective 15 April 2014), companies with net worth ≥ ₹1500 Cr OR turnover ≥ ₹1000 Cr OR net profit ≥ ₹15 Cr must spend at least 2% of average net profit of last 3 years on CSR activities. CSR committee of 4 directors required. Activities include eradicating poverty, promoting education, environmental sustainability, rural development, etc.
