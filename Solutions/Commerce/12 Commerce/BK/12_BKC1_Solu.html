Q.C State True or False with reasons

1) Partnership firm is a Non Trading Concern.
False Partnership firm is a trading concern as it carries on business with profit motive.
2) Profit and Loss Account is a Real Account.
False Profit & Loss Account is a Nominal Account.
3) Carriage Inward is carriage on purchases.
True Carriage Inward is incurred to bring goods to business premises.
4) Adjustments are recorded in Partners Current Account in Fixed Capital Method.
True In Fixed Capital Method, adjustments are made through Current Account.
5) Prepaid expenses are treated as liabilities.
False Prepaid expenses are shown as Assets in Balance Sheet.
6) If Partnership Deed is silent, partners share profits in proportion to their capital.
False They share profits equally in absence of partnership deed.
7) Balance sheet is an Account.
False Balance Sheet is a statement, not an account.
8) Wages paid for installation of Machinery is a Revenue expenditure.
False It is Capital expenditure and debited to Machinery A/c.
9) Income received in advance is a liability.
True It is shown on liability side as it is earned for next period.
10) R.D.D is created on creditors.
False R.D.D. (Reserve for Doubtful Debts) is created on Debtors.

Q.F Answer in one sentence only

1) What is fluctuating capital?
Fluctuating capital is a method where capital balance changes every year due to adjustments recorded directly in Capital Account.
2) Why is Partnership Deed necessary?
Partnership Deed is necessary to avoid disputes and clearly define rights, duties, and profit sharing ratio among partners.
3) If Partnership Deed is silent, in which ratio will partners share profit or loss?
Partners will share profits and losses equally.
4) What is Fixed Capital Method?
Fixed Capital Method is where capital balances remain constant and all adjustments are recorded in separate Current Account.
5) How many partners are required to form a Partnership Firm?
Minimum 2 partners are required to form a partnership firm.
6) What is Partnership Deed?
Partnership Deed is a written document containing all terms and conditions of partnership agreement.
7) What rate of interest is allowed on partner's loan in absence of agreement?
6% per annum interest is allowed on partner's loan.
8) What is minimum number of partners as per Indian Partnership Act, 1932?
Minimum 2 partners are required.
9) What is liability of a partner?
Liability of a partner is unlimited, joint and several.
10) What is pre-received income?
Pre-received income is income received in advance for the next accounting period.

Q.H Calculate the following

1) Undervaluation of Closing Stock by 10%. Closing Stock was ₹30,000. Find actual value.
If undervalued by 10%, actual = 30,000 × (100/90) = ₹33,333
2) Calculate 12.5% depreciation on Furniture: a) on ₹2,20,000 for 1 year b) on ₹10,000 for 6 months.
a) 2,20,000 × 12.5% = ₹27,500; b) 10,000 × 12.5% × 6/12 = ₹625
3) Insurance Premium ₹1,500 paid for year ending 1st Sep 2019. Calculate prepaid for year ending 31st March 2019.
Period from 1st Apr to 31st Mar = 12 months; paid up to 1st Sep = 5 months used; Prepaid for 7 months = 1,500 × 7/12 = ₹875
4) Find Gross Profit: Purchases 30,000, Sales 15,000, Carriage Inward 2,400, Opening Stock 10,000, Purchase Returns 1,000, Closing Stock 36,000.
Cost of Goods Sold = 10,000 + (30,000-1,000) + 2,400 = 41,400; Gross Loss = 15,000 - 41,400 = ₹26,400 (Gross Loss)
5) Loan ₹2,00,000 taken on 1st Oct 2019 @15% p.a. Calculate interest for year ending 31st March 2020.
Period = 6 months; Interest = 2,00,000 × 15% × 6/12 = ₹15,000

Interest on Drawings - Important Note

When date of drawings is not given: Interest is calculated for average period of 6 months.
Formula: Drawings × Rate/100 × 6/12
When fixed amount is withdrawn on same date each month: Interest = Total Drawings × Rate/100 × 5.5/12
