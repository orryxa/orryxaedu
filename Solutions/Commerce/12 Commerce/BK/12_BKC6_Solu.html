Chapter 6 · Dissolution of Partnership Firm

📘 Chapter Summary: Dissolution of Partnership Firm

🔹 Meaning: Dissolution means complete closure of partnership business. As per Indian Partnership Act 1932, Sec. 39 — dissolution of partnership between all partners is called dissolution of firm. All assets are realized, liabilities paid, and books closed permanently.

📌 Key Point: Dissolution of Partnership ≠ Dissolution of Firm. Partnership may continue with reconstitution, but Firm dissolution ends everything.

🔹 Settlement Order (Sec.48):
1️⃣ Realisation expenses → 2️⃣ Outside liabilities → 3️⃣ Partners' loans → 4️⃣ Partners' capital (in profit sharing ratio).

🔹 Key Accounts: Realisation A/c (Nominal A/c) – transfers all assets (except cash) & third-party liabilities; shows profit/loss on dissolution. Partners' Capital A/c – adjusted for reserves, revaluation profit/loss, and final settlement.

🔹 Insolvency of Partner: If a partner has debit balance (deficiency) and is insolvent, the loss is borne by solvent partners in their profit sharing ratio.

🔹 When ALL partners are insolvent: Third party liabilities are not transferred to Realisation A/c. Deficiency A/c is opened to close the books after distributing available cash proportionately.

⭐ Important Journal Entries: Transfer of assets & liabilities to Realisation A/c, sale of assets, payment to creditors, transfer of realisation profit/loss to partners' capital, final settlement.
