Chapter 8 · Company Accounts: Issue of Shares

📘 Chapter Summary: Issue of Shares

🔹 Share & Share Capital: Share is the smallest unit of company's capital. Share Capital is the capital raised by issuing shares. Types: Authorised Capital (maximum), Issued Capital (offered), Subscribed Capital (actually taken), Called-up Capital (demanded), Paid-up Capital (received), Calls in Arrears (unpaid), Reserve Capital (called only on winding up).

📌 Key Point: Equity Shares (risk bearers, variable dividend) vs Preference Shares (fixed dividend, priority in repayment).

🔹 Issue at Par, Premium & Discount: At Par (face value). At Premium (issue price > face value; premium credited to Securities Premium A/c). At Discount (issue price < face value; discount is loss; not allowed for public issue now).

🔹 Over/Under Subscription & Pro-rata Allotment: Over subscription → applications > shares; reject, refund, or pro-rata allotment. Under subscription → applications < shares; allot only applied shares. Pro-rata → allotment proportionately, excess application money adjusted toward allotment.

🔹 Forfeiture & Re-issue: Shares forfeited for non-payment of calls. Share Capital A/c debited (called-up amount), Share Forfeiture A/c credited (amount received). Re-issue at discount (≤ forfeited amount). Balance transferred to Capital Reserve.

⭐ Important Journal Entries: Application receipt & transfer, Allotment due & receipt, Calls due & receipt, Calls in Arrears, Forfeiture, Re-issue, Transfer to Capital Reserve.

Illustration: Over Subscription & Pro-rata Allotment

Company issued 10,000 shares @ ₹10 (₹2 app, ₹5 allot, ₹3 call). Applications for 20,000 shares. Pro-rata allotment. Excess application money adjusted with allotment.

Working: Application received = 20,000×2=40,000; Transfer to capital = 10,000×2=20,000; Excess adjusted = 20,000 towards allotment. Allotment due = 10,000×5=50,000; Net allotment received = 50,000 - 20,000 = 30,000.

G. Calculate the following

1) 500 shares: app ₹3, allot ₹4 paid, final call ₹3 unpaid. Forfeiture amount?
Amount received = 500×(3+4)= ₹3,500
2) 10,000 shares ₹10 each at 10% premium. Total premium?
10,000 × 10 × 10% = ₹10,000
3) Excess app 5,000 shares @₹4. 1,000 rejected, pro-rata. Application money adjusted?
Excess = 5,000×4=20,000; Refund = 1,000×4=4,000; Adjusted = 16,000
4) 80,000 shares ₹10 each issued & fully subscribed at 20% premium. Equity Share Capital?
80,000 × 10 = ₹8,00,000 (Premium separate)
5) 20,000 shares ₹100 each at par, fully called. 100 shares failed final call of ₹20. Paid-up capital?
(20,000×100) - (100×20) = 20,00,000 - 2,000 = ₹19,98,000

E. Answer in one sentence only

1) What are Preference shares?
Shares with preferential right to dividend and capital repayment.
2) What is Registered/Nominal Capital?
Maximum capital company can issue as per Memorandum.
3) What is Reserve Capital?
Part of subscribed capital not called up except at winding up.

When applications received exceed shares offered.

Bank A/c is debited.
