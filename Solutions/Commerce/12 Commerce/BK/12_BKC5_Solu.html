C. State True or False with reasons

1) Deceased partner is not entitled to Goodwill of the firm.
False Deceased partner is entitled to his share of goodwill as per partnership deed.
2) Deceased partner is entitled to his share of General Reserve.
True General Reserve is distributed to all partners including deceased in old ratio.
3) If Goodwill is written off, deceased partner's capital account is debited.
True When goodwill is written off, all partners' capital accounts are debited in new ratio.
4) Entire amount due to deceased partner is paid to legal representative immediately.
False Amount is transferred to Executor's Loan Account and paid after formalities.
5) Profit/Loss up to death is recorded in Profit & Loss Appropriation Account.
False It is recorded in Profit & Loss Suspense Account.

E. Answer in one sentence only

1) What is gain ratio?
The ratio in which continuing partners acquire the deceased partner's share = New Ratio - Old Ratio.
2) In which ratio is General Reserve distributed on death of a partner?
In the old profit sharing ratio (including deceased partner).
3) To whom is General Reserve distributed on death of a partner?
To all partners including deceased partner in their old profit sharing ratio.
4) How is death of a partner a compulsory retirement?
Death automatically terminates the partnership relation of that partner.
5) To which account is profit up to death transferred?
Profit & Loss Suspense Account.

Important Formulas

Gain Ratio (Death)
Gain = New Ratio - Old Ratio
Goodwill Valuation
Average Profit = Total Profit / No. of years
Goodwill = Average Profit × Years Purchase
Deceased Partner's Share = Goodwill × Old Ratio
Profit up to Death
Average Profit of last 2/3 years × Period (months/12) × Deceased Partner's Share
Interest on Capital (up to death)
Capital × Rate × Period/12
Salary up to Death
Monthly Salary × Number of months
