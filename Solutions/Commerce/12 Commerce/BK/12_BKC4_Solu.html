C. State True or False with reasons

1) Gain ratio = New ratio - Old ratio.
True Gain ratio is the excess of new ratio over old ratio for continuing partners.
2) Retiring partner's share of profit up to retirement date is debited to P&L Suspense A/c.
True P&L Suspense A/c is debited and retiring partner's capital is credited.
3) On retirement, sacrifice ratio is considered.
False On retirement, gain ratio is considered; sacrifice ratio is at admission.
4) Retiring partner is called an outgoing partner.
True Partner who leaves the firm is outgoing partner.
5) Remaining partners share goodwill in their new profit sharing ratio.
True Continuing partners share retiring partner's goodwill in gain ratio.
6) Retiring partner is not entitled to share in General Reserve.
False Retiring partner gets his share of General Reserve in old ratio.

E. Answer the following

1) What is Retirement of a Partner?
When a partner ceases to be a partner and remaining partners continue business, it is called retirement.
2) What is Benefit/Gain Ratio?
Ratio in which continuing partners acquire retiring partner's share = New Ratio - Old Ratio.
3) What is New Ratio?
Ratio in which continuing partners decide to share future profits and losses after retirement.
4) How is amount due to retiring partner settled?
By cash payment in lump sum, installments, or transferred to Loan Account.
5) How is Gain Ratio calculated?
Gain Ratio = New Ratio - Old Ratio (for continuing partners).
6) Why is retiring partner's capital credited with goodwill?
Because goodwill represents firm's reputation earned by past efforts of all partners including retiring partner.

Important Formulas

New Ratio (when no specific acquisition)
Cancel retiring partner's share from old ratio
Gain Ratio
Gain = New Ratio - Old Ratio
New Ratio = Old Ratio + Gain Ratio
Revaluation Profit/Loss
Distributed to ALL partners (including retiring) in OLD ratio
Reserves/Accumulated Profit
Distributed to ALL partners in OLD ratio
Goodwill (Retirement)
Retiring partner's share credited to him; continuing partners debit in GAIN ratio

Illustration: Gain Ratio Calculation

Old Ratio (A:B:C = 4:3:2). B retires. A and C share future equally (1:1).
A's Gain = 1/2 - 4/9 = (9-8)/18 = 1/18
C's Gain = 1/2 - 2/9 = (9-4)/18 = 5/18
Gain Ratio (A:C) = 1:5
