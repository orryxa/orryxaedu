C. State True or False with reasons

1) Not for Profit Concerns do not have profit motive.
True Their primary objective is to provide service, not to earn profit.
2) Charitable Institutions prepare Profit and Loss Account.
False They prepare Income and Expenditure Account.
3) Receipts and Payments Account and Income and Expenditure Account are same.
False R&P A/c is real account (cash basis); I&E A/c is nominal account (accrual basis).
4) Income and Expenditure Account represents either surplus or deficit.
True Surplus if income > expenditure; Deficit if expenditure > income.
5) Receipts and Payments Account has no opening balance.
False It starts with opening cash and bank balances.
6) Not for Profit Concerns do not prepare Balance Sheet.
False They prepare Balance Sheet to show financial position.
7) Purchase of Sports Equipment is a Capital Expenditure.
True It is an asset purchased for long-term use.
8) Income and Expenditure Account is a Real Account.
False It is a Nominal Account.
9) Receipts and Payments Account contains only current year transactions.
False It includes previous, current and next year transactions.
10) Excess of Assets over Liabilities is called Capital Fund.
True Capital Fund = Total Assets - Total Liabilities.

E. Answer in one sentence only

1) What do you mean by 'Not for Profit Concern'?
Organizations established to provide social service without profit motive, e.g., clubs, hospitals, schools.
2) Which organizations prepare Income and Expenditure Account?
Not for Profit concerns like schools, colleges, clubs, hospitals, trusts prepare Income and Expenditure Account.
3) What is Receipts and Payments Account?
A summary of all cash and bank receipts and payments for a particular period, irrespective of revenue/capital nature.
4) Why Income and Expenditure Account is prepared?
To ascertain surplus or deficit of a Not for Profit concern for an accounting year.
5) What is Capital Fund?
Excess of total assets over total liabilities of a Not for Profit concern.
6) What is Subscription?
Annual fees paid by members to Not for Profit concern, a major revenue source.
7) What is Legacy?
Gift received under a will on death of a donor.
8) What is Surplus?
Excess of income over expenditure in Income and Expenditure Account.
9) What do you mean by Non Recurring Expenses?
Expenses incurred occasionally, not forming part of regular operations (Capital Expenditure).
10) To which account Surplus or Deficit is transferred?
Surplus or Deficit is transferred to Capital Fund in Balance Sheet.

Distinguish between: Receipts & Payments A/c and Income & Expenditure A/c

PointReceipts & Payments AccountIncome & Expenditure Account
Type of AccountReal AccountNominal Account
NatureSimilar to Cash BookSimilar to Profit & Loss A/c
Opening BalanceHas opening cash/bank balanceNo opening balance
BasisCash basisAccrual basis
RecordsAll receipts & payments (Revenue + Capital)Only revenue incomes & expenses
Non-cash itemsNot recordedRecorded (depreciation, outstanding, prepaid)
Closing balanceCash and Bank balanceSurplus or Deficit

Types of Receipts and Payments

Capital Receipts
• Non-recurring
• e.g., Donation for Building, Life Membership Fees, Sale of Fixed Asset, Legacies
• Added to Capital Fund / Liability side
Revenue Receipts
• Recurring
• e.g., Subscription, Entrance Fees (if revenue), Rent, Interest
• Credited to Income & Expenditure A/c
Capital Expenditure
• Non-recurring
• e.g., Purchase of Building, Furniture, Equipment
• Shown on Asset side of Balance Sheet
Revenue Expenditure
• Recurring
• e.g., Salaries, Rent, Printing, Stationery
• Debited to Income & Expenditure A/c
