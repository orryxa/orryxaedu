C. State True or False with reasons

1) New Partner can bring capital in cash or kind.
True Capital can be brought in cash or as assets (kind).
2) When goodwill is paid privately, it is not recorded in books.
True Private arrangement does not affect firm's books.
3) Gain ratio is calculated at the time of admission.
False Sacrifice ratio is calculated at admission; gain ratio at retirement.
4) Revaluation profit is distributed among all partners including new partner.
False Revaluation profit/loss belongs to old partners only (old ratio).
5) Change in relationship between partners is Reconstitution.
True Any change in partnership agreement leads to reconstitution.
6) New partner always brings his share of goodwill in cash.
False Goodwill can be brought in cash or adjusted through capital.
7) When goodwill is written off, goodwill account is debited.
False When goodwill is written off, partners' capital is debited and goodwill credited.
8) New ratio minus old ratio equals sacrifice ratio.
False Old ratio minus new ratio = sacrifice ratio.
9) Usually at admission, capital of firm increases.
True New partner brings additional capital.
10) Cash/Bank account is credited when goodwill is withdrawn by old partners.
True When goodwill amount is withdrawn, Cash/Bank is credited.

F. Answer the following

1) What is Revaluation Account?
It is a nominal account opened to record changes in value of assets and liabilities at admission/retirement, and profit/loss is transferred to old partners in old ratio.
2) What is Reconstitution of partnership?
Change in relationship/agreement among partners due to admission, retirement, death, or change in profit sharing ratio.
3) Why is new partner admitted?
To increase capital, bring skills, expand business, or improve management.
4) What is sacrifice ratio?
Ratio in which old partners sacrifice their share of profit in favour of new partner = Old Ratio - New Ratio.
5) What is raising goodwill at admission?
Valuation Method where goodwill is raised in books and then written off among all partners in new ratio.

Important Formulas

Average Profit Method
Goodwill = Average Profit × Number of years purchase
Super Profit Method
Super Profit = Average Profit - Normal Profit
Normal Profit = Capital Employed × NRR/100
Goodwill = Super Profit × Years Purchase
New Ratio Calculation
If new partner's share = 1/m, Remaining = (m-1)/m
New ratio = Old Ratio × Remaining share
Sacrifice Ratio
Sacrifice Ratio = Old Ratio - New Ratio
Capital Adjustment
Total Capital = New Partner's Capital × Reciprocal of his share
