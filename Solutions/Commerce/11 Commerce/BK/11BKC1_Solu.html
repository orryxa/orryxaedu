Foundations of Book-keeping & Accountancy

Q.1 Answer in One Sentence

1) What is Book-keeping ?
Book-keeping is the art of recording business transactions in a systematic manner.
2) What is meant by Goods?
Goods are commodities or articles in which a trader deals.
3) What is Capital?
Capital is the amount invested in the business by the proprietor.
4) What is Drawings?
Drawings are cash or goods withdrawn by the owner for personal use.
5) What is Goodwill?
Goodwill is the reputation of a business expressed in terms of money.

Q.4 State Whether True or False with Reasons

1) Book-keeping and accounting are one and the same thing.
False Book-keeping records transactions whereas accounting includes summarizing, analysis and interpretation.
2) Conservatism means to follow safe side.
True Conservatism means anticipating losses but not profits.
3) The double entry system is based on “Dual Aspect” concept.
True Every debit has a corresponding credit.
4) Bank overdraft is an asset of the business.
False Bank overdraft is a liability of the business.
5) Solvent person is a person whose assets are more than liabilities.
True Such a person can pay all obligations.
6) Cash discount does not appear in the books of accounts.
False Cash discount appears in the books of accounts.
7) A transaction is concerned with money or money’s worth.
True Transactions involve exchange of money or money’s worth.
8) Accounting is the language of business.
True Accounting communicates business information.
9) In civilization, accounting was done by owners.
False Earlier accounting was done by agents.
10) Book-keeping is useful to find out all tax liabilities.
True It helps in calculating taxes like GST and Income Tax.

Q.5 Do You Agree or Disagree

1) Accounting is useful only to the owner.
Disagree - Accounting is useful to owners, investors, government, lenders and management.
2) Book-keeping is an art, science and profession.
Disagree - Book-keeping is mainly an art and science.
3) Bills Payable is an asset of the business.
Disagree - Bills Payable is a liability.
4) In Book-keeping and Accountancy only non monetary transactions are recorded.
Disagree - Only monetary transactions are recorded.
5) Totalling of Journal or Ledger is called as casting.
Agree - Totalling is known as casting.
