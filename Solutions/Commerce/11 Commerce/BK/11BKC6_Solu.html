Q.1 Answer in One Sentence

1) Who prepares a bank Pass Book?
Bank prepares the Pass Book (or Bank Statement) for its customer.
2) What is pay-in-slip?
Pay-in-slip is a form filled by account holder to deposit cash or cheque into bank account.
3) What is bank overdraft?
Bank overdraft is a facility where account holder can withdraw more than the available balance (credit balance as per Cash Book).
4) What is withdrawal slip?
Withdrawal slip is a form used to withdraw cash from bank account over the counter.
5) Who sends the bank statement?
Bank sends the bank statement to account holder periodically.
6) What does a debit balance in Cash Book represent?
Debit balance in Cash Book represents favourable bank balance (cash at bank).
7) Who prepares the Bank Reconciliation Statement?
Businessman (account holder) prepares the Bank Reconciliation Statement.
8) What does debit balance in Pass Book represent?
Debit balance in Pass Book represents bank overdraft (unfavourable balance).
9) On which side is interest on bank deposit recorded in Pass Book?
Interest on bank deposit is recorded on credit side (deposit column) of Pass Book.
10) Why is Bank Reconciliation Statement prepared?
To reconcile the difference between Cash Book balance and Pass Book balance and detect errors.

Q.3 Do you agree or disagree?

1) The bank column of Cash Book represents bank account.
Agree Bank column in Cash Book is a ledger account of bank.
2) Bank statement enables account holder to prepare Bank Reconciliation Statement.
Agree BRS is prepared by comparing Cash Book with Bank Statement.
3) Cheques issued for payment but not presented to bank appears in Cash Book only.
Agree Such cheques are recorded in Cash Book but not yet in Pass Book.
4) Bank Reconciliation Statement is prepared only during the year end.
Disagree It can be prepared periodically (monthly/quarterly) as per requirement.
5) Bank Reconciliation Statement is similar to bank statement.
Disagree Bank statement is prepared by bank; BRS is prepared by businessman.
6) Bank balance as per Cash Book is always equal to bank balance as per Pass Book.
Disagree They often differ due to timing and errors.
7) Bank advice is sent by the businessman to bank.
Disagree Bank advice is sent by bank to businessman.
8) Pay-in-slip is used for depositing cheque into bank.
Agree It is used for depositing both cash and cheques.
9) Difference in Cash Book balance and Pass Book balance may arise due to errors committed while recording.
Agree Errors like wrong amount, omission cause difference.
10) Payment and receipt of cash through internet banking generates automatic proof.
Agree Online transactions provide instant digital evidence.

Q.6 State True or False with reasons

1) Cheques deposited into bank but not yet cleared appears in the Pass Book only.
False Such cheques appear in Cash Book (deposited) but not yet credited in Pass Book.
2) Direct deposit made by debtors into businessman's bank account is recorded on the credit side of Pass Book.
True It increases bank balance, hence credited in Pass Book.
3) Businessman can prepare Bank Reconciliation statement only with Cash Book Balance.
False BRS can be prepared starting with either Cash Book balance or Pass Book balance.
4) When overdraft as per Cash Book is given, bank charges debited in Pass Book only, is to be added.
True Bank charges reduce overdraft, so added to overdraft as per Cash Book.
5) Bank Statement is sent by Bank to businessman.
True Bank issues periodic statement to account holder.

Causes of difference between Cash Book and Pass Book

Due to time difference:
✓ Cheque issued but not presented
✓ Cheque deposited but not cleared
✓ Interest credited by bank
✓ Direct collection by bank
✓ Direct payment by bank
✓ Bank charges, commission
✓ Dishonour of cheques
✓ Amount directly deposited
Due to errors:
✓ Recording on wrong side
✓ Wrong amount recorded
✓ Wrong balancing / totalling
✓ Double recording
✓ Omission of transaction
