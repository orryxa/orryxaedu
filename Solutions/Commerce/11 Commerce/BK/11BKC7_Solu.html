Q.1 Answer in One Sentence

1) What is depreciation?
Depreciation is a gradual, continuous and permanent reduction in the value of a fixed asset due to wear and tear, passage of time or obsolescence.
2) Why depreciation is charged?
Depreciation is charged to ascertain true profit or loss and to show true financial position of the business.
3) What is a 'Scrap Value' of an asset?
Scrap value is the estimated realizable value of an asset at the end of its useful life.
4) Why depreciation is charged even in the year of loss?
Depreciation is a non-cash expense and must be charged regardless of profit or loss to reflect correct asset value.
5) Which account is credited when depreciation is charged?
Asset account is credited when depreciation is charged.
6) Where is the profit or loss on sale of asset transferred?
Profit or loss on sale of asset is transferred to Profit & Loss Account.
7) To which account balance on Depreciation A/c is transferred?
Balance of Depreciation A/c is transferred to Profit & Loss A/c.
8) What is the formula to calculate depreciation by Straight Line Method?
Depreciation p.a. = (Original Cost - Scrap Value) / Estimated Life of Asset (in years).
9) What is Fixed Instalment Method?
Fixed Instalment Method (Straight Line Method) charges equal amount of depreciation every year on original cost.
10) Which account is debited when expenses are paid on installation of Machinery?
Machinery A/c (Asset A/c) is debited for installation expenses.

Q.8 Calculate the following

1) Machine costing ₹23,000, life 7 years, scrap value ₹2,000. Find depreciation p.a.
Depreciation = (23,000 - 2,000) / 7 = ₹21,000 / 7 = ₹3,000 p.a.
2) Computer cost ₹40,000, depreciation @8% p.a. Calculate depreciation.
Depreciation = 40,000 × 8/100 = ₹3,200 p.a.
3) Furniture purchased 1st Oct 2015 for ₹2,80,000 + installation ₹20,000, depreciation @6% SLM on 31st March 2016.
Cost = ₹3,00,000; Period = 6 months; Depreciation = 3,00,000 × 6% × 6/12 = ₹9,000
4) Machinery purchased 1st Jan 2016 for ₹2,00,000, depreciation @10% WDV, books closed 31st March. Find WDV as on 31st March 2017.
For 2015-16 (3 months): Dep = 2,00,000 × 10% × 3/12 = ₹5,000; WDV = ₹1,95,000.
For 2016-17: Dep = 1,95,000 × 10% = ₹19,500; WDV on 31/3/17 = ₹1,75,500
5) Machinery sold for ₹7,000 on 1st July 2016 (original cost ₹10,000 purchased 1st April 2015), depreciation @10% SLM. Find profit/loss.
Depreciation 2015-16: ₹1,000; 2016-17 (3 months): ₹250; Total depreciation = ₹1,250; WDV = ₹8,750; Sale price ₹7,000 → Loss = ₹1,750

Depreciation is a non-cash expense; it reduces asset value and is charged to P&L A/c.

Causes of Depreciation

1. Wear and Tear
Due to regular use of asset.
2. Passage of Time
Assets like patents, copyrights lose value over time.
3. Obsolescence
Technological advancements make old assets outdated.
4. Depletion
Wasting assets like mines, quarries.
5. Natural Calamities
Fire, floods, earthquakes, accidents.
6. Market Value Changes
Fluctuations in market price.

Important Formulas

Straight Line Method (SLM)
Depreciation p.a. = (Original Cost - Scrap Value) / Estimated Life (years)
SLM (when rate given)
Depreciation p.a. = Original Cost × Rate of Depreciation / 100
Written Down Value Method (WDV)
Depreciation = Opening WDV × Rate / 100
Original Cost of Asset
Purchase Price + Installation Charges + Freight + Brokerage + Any incidental cost
Profit/Loss on Sale of Asset
Selling Price - Written Down Value on date of sale (Profit if positive, Loss if negative)

Practical Problem: Depreciation under WDV Method

Asset Cost: ₹80,000 | Rate: 10% p.a. | WDV Method
Year 1: Dep = 80,000 × 10% = ₹8,000 → WDV = ₹72,000
Year 2: Dep = 72,000 × 10% = ₹7,200 → WDV = ₹64,800
Year 3: Dep = 64,800 × 10% = ₹6,480 → WDV = ₹58,320
Year 4: Dep = 58,320 × 10% = ₹5,832 → WDV = ₹52,488
