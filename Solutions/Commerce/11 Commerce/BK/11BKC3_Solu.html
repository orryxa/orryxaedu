Q.1 Answer in One Sentence

1) What is Journal?
Journal is a book of original entry where business transactions are recorded chronologically with debit and credit aspects.
2) What is Narration?
Narration is a brief explanation of a journal entry written below the entry within brackets.
3) What is GST?
GST (Goods and Services Tax) is a comprehensive indirect tax levied on supply of goods and services, introduced on 1st July 2017.
4) In which year GST was imposed by Central Government of India?
GST was imposed from 1st July 2017.
5) What is meant by simple entry?
A simple journal entry involves only two accounts, one debit and one credit.
6) What is the meaning of combined entry?
Combined (compound) entry has more than one debit or more than one credit or both.
7) Which account is debited, when rent is paid by Debit card?
Rent A/c is debited and Bank A/c is credited.
8) Which discount is not recorded in the books of account?
Trade discount is not recorded in the books of accounts.
9) In which order monthly transactions are recorded in a Journal?
Transactions are recorded in chronological order (date-wise).
10) Which account is credited, when goods are sold on credit?
Sales A/c is credited.

Q.4 State True or False with reasons

1) Narration is not required for each and every entry.
False Narration is required for every journal entry to explain the transaction.
2) A journal voucher is must for all transactions recorded in the Journal.
True Every journal entry is supported by a journal voucher as documentary evidence.
3) Cash discount allowed should be debited to discount A/c.
True Discount allowed is an expense/loss, hence debited.
4) Journal is a book of prime entry.
True Journal is the first book where transactions are recorded.
5) Trade discount is recorded in the books of accounts.
False Trade discount is not recorded; only net amount after trade discount is recorded.
6) Goods lost by theft is debited to goods A/c.
False Loss by theft A/c is debited, Purchases A/c is credited.
7) If rent is paid to landlord, landlord's A/c should be debited.
False Rent A/c is debited (expense), landlord is not directly debited.
8) Book Keeping records monetary and non-monetary transactions.
False Only monetary transactions are recorded.
9) Drawings made by the proprietor increases his capital.
False Drawings reduce the capital.
10) GST paid on purchase of goods Input tax A/c should be debited.
True Input CGST & Input SGST are debited (asset for set-off).

Q.9 Numerical Calculations (GST & Discount)

1) Purchased Motor Car ₹2,00,000 at 18% GST. Find GST amount.
GST = 2,00,000 × 18% = ₹36,000 (CGST ₹18,000, SGST ₹18,000)
2) Transport charges ₹10,000 @5% GST. Calculate CGST & SGST.
CGST = 2.5% = ₹250, SGST = 2.5% = ₹250, Total GST = ₹500
3) Bought goods ₹10,000 @5% GST and 10% cash discount. Calculate cash discount.
Net of GST? Cash discount is calculated on invoice price (excluding GST usually). Here cash discount = ₹10,000 × 10% = ₹1,000.
4) Received cheque ₹90,000 in full settlement of ₹1,00,000. Calculate discount rate.
Discount = ₹10,000, Rate = (10,000/1,00,000)×100 = 10%
5) Sold goods ₹1,00,000 at 10% Trade discount & 10% cash discount, received 50% amount by cheque.
After trade discount = ₹90,000; cash discount on 50% = 90,000×50%×10% = ₹4,500; Cheque received = (45,000 - 4,500) = ₹40,500.

Q.7 Correct the following statements

1) All business transactions are recorded in the Journal.
Only monetary transactions are recorded in the Journal.
2) Cash discount is not recorded in the books of accounts.
Cash discount is recorded in the books of accounts.
3) Journal is a book of Secondary entry.
Journal is a book of Prime entry (original entry).
4) GST is imposed by Government of India from 1st July 2018.
GST imposed from 1st July 2017.
5) Machinery purchased by the Proprietor decreases his capital.
Machinery purchased increases assets but capital remains same (unless financed by capital).

Practical Problem (Illustrative Journal Entry)

Journalise: Started business with Cash ₹60,000, Bank ₹1,00,000, Goods ₹50,000.
Cash A/c Dr. 60,000; Bank A/c Dr. 1,00,000; Stock A/c Dr. 50,000 → To Capital A/c 2,10,000
Purchased goods from Mohan ₹80,000 at 10% Trade Discount.
Purchases A/c Dr. 72,000 → To Mohan A/c 72,000
Sold goods to Urmila ₹30,000 at 5% Trade Discount.
Urmila A/c Dr. 28,500 → To Sales A/c 28,500
Paid Rent ₹4,000 by debit card.
Rent A/c Dr. 4,000 → To Bank A/c 4,000
Goods distributed as free sample ₹5,000.
Advertisement A/c Dr. 5,000 → To Purchases A/c 5,000
Note: Trade discount is deducted before recording; GST entries require Input/Output CGST & SGST splits.
