Overall Summary

Official textbook chapter (Maharashtra Board / 11th BK) – “Subsidiary Books”. Covers Cash Book (single, two-column), Petty Cash Book (imprest system), Purchase/Sales Books, Returns Books, and Journal Proper. Includes theory, formats, solved illustrations, and unsolved exercises.

1) Need & types of subsidiary books 2) Simple & two-column Cash Book (Cash+Bank) 3) Contra entries & banking transactions 4) Petty Cash Book (Analytical) under imprest system 5) Purchase, Sales, Purchase Return, Sales Return Books 6) Journal Proper (opening, adjusting, closing, rectification entries) 7) GST-integrated purchase/sales books.
Competency statements
Students will understand subsidiary books, classify cash/credit transactions, prepare various cash books, record contra entries, and maintain special journals like purchase/sales books and journal proper.
Strength: Excellent blend of conceptual clarity with step-by-step numerical problems. Includes GST adjustments, trade discount treatments, and real-world banking scenarios (dishonoured cheques, direct deposits, bank charges).

From simple cash book → two-column cash book → petty cash → purchase/sales books → journal proper. Builds competency logically.

Bearer cheque vs crossed cheque, overdraft, direct deposit by customer, standing instructions, bank charges, interest on overdraft – fully explained.
Contra entries clearly marked
Cash deposited/withdrawn from bank shown with 'C' in L.F. column – practical format used in industry.

Weaknesses / Gaps

1) Missing triple-column cash book
Gap Only cash + bank columns covered. No discount column (cash discount column) which is common in many curricula. Students may need separate reference for three-column cash book.
2) Page formatting & scanning issues
OCR/scan Some pages (e.g., page 19) have repetitive placeholder text ("XXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXX") due to scanning artifacts. Not a conceptual issue but affects readability.
3) Limited coverage of cash discount in special books
Cash discount treatment mentioned in Journal Proper but not integrated into Sales/Purchase Books. Modern entries often record discount separately – could be more explicit.
4) No electronic / digital subsidiary books
Entirely manual bookkeeping approach. Does not introduce accounting software or ERP concepts (e.g., Tally, Zoho Books) which is fine for 11th standard but could add contemporary relevance.
5) Overdraft illustration complexity
Problem 4 (Guru Dakshina Brothers) contains advanced overdraft with multiple contra entries. While comprehensive, beginners may find balancing overdraft in bank column tricky without additional explanation.

Format clarity: Dr./Cr. sides clearly labelled, narration included in most entries. L.F. (Ledger Folio) column present – exam-friendly.
Trade discount vs cash discount: Separate sections explain trade discount never recorded, only net amount. Cash discount recorded in journal proper.
Bank account types: Current, Savings, Fixed, Recurring – summarized nicely. Overdraft facility explained in context of current account.
Journal Proper coverage: Opening, adjusting, closing, rectification, and transfer entries – each with solved examples. Rectification of errors included.
Data representation
All financial figures in ₹ (Indian Rupees). Trade discount percentages, GST rates (9% CGST+9% SGST or 18% IGST) are realistic. Tables for purchase/sales books include columns for CGST/SGST/IGST – advanced analytical format.
Teaching methodology: Each subsidiary book is introduced with meaning → format → rules → solved problem → posting to ledger → trial balance (where relevant). Excellent scaffolding for students.

Key Rules / Golden Points from Chapter

Trade discount rule
Never recorded in books. Only net amount (after deducting trade discount) is recorded in Purchase/Sales book.
Contra entry condition
Affects both cash and bank accounts simultaneously (e.g., cash deposited into bank). Marked with 'C' in L.F. column.
Imprest system formula
Imprest amount = fixed advance. At end of period: reimbursement = total petty expenses paid. Petty cashier always starts with same imprest amount.
Purchase Book inclusion
Only credit purchases of goods (trading stock). Cash purchases → Cash Book. Asset purchases on credit → Journal Proper.
Purchase Return Book (Returns outward)
Recorded using Debit Note issued to supplier. Supplier issues Credit Note.
Journal Proper – 5 categories
Opening entries, adjustment entries, closing entries, transfer entries, rectification entries + other (discount, drawings of goods, asset sale on credit).
Memorization aid: “Debit what comes in, credit what goes out” (for Cash Book). For Sales/Purchase books: “All credit sales of goods → Sales Book; All credit purchases of goods → Purchase Book”. Returns are reverse but with Debit/Credit notes.
