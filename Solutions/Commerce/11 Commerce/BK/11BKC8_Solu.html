Q.1 Answer in One Sentence

1) What is meant by rectification of errors?
Rectification of errors means correcting the mistakes committed in the books of accounts by passing necessary journal entries or by adjusting the accounts.
2) What is meant by error of principle?
Error of principle occurs when a transaction is recorded against the accounting principles, such as treating capital expenditure as revenue expenditure.
3) What is meant by error of partial omission?
Error of partial omission occurs when a transaction is partially recorded, e.g., recorded in Journal but not posted to Ledger, which affects Trial Balance agreement.
4) What is meant by error of complete omission?
Error of complete omission occurs when a transaction is completely omitted from the books of accounts, which does not affect Trial Balance agreement.
5) What are compensating errors?
Compensating errors are two or more errors whose effect cancels each other, resulting in no impact on Trial Balance agreement.

Q.4 State True or False with reasons

1) Trial Balance is prepared from the balance of ledger accounts.
True Trial Balance lists all ledger account balances to check arithmetical accuracy.
2) A Trial Balance can agree in spite of certain errors.
True Errors like complete omission, compensating errors, errors of principle do not affect Trial Balance agreement.
3) Rectification entries are passed in Cash Book.
False Rectification entries are passed in Journal Proper.
4) There is no need to open a Suspense Account if the Trial Balance agrees.
True Suspense Account is opened only when Trial Balance does not agree.
5) All the errors can be rectified only through Suspense Account.
False Only one-sided errors after Trial Balance are rectified through Suspense Account; two-sided errors are rectified by journal entries directly.

Rectification Entry Principle

Rectifying Entry = Reverse Wrong Entry + Pass Correct Entry
For one-sided errors after Trial Balance: Use Suspense Account.
For two-sided errors: Direct Journal Entry in Journal Proper.

One-sided vs Two-sided Errors

One-sided Errors
• Affect only one account
• Affect Trial Balance agreement
• Rectified through Suspense Account (if after Trial Balance)
• Example: Wrong totalling, omission of posting
Two-sided Errors
• Affect two or more accounts
• Do NOT affect Trial Balance agreement
• Rectified directly by Journal Entry in Journal Proper
• Example: Complete omission, error of principle

Steps for Rectification of Errors

Step 1: Identify the wrong entry passed (if any).
Step 2: Determine the correct entry that should have been passed.
Step 3: Reverse the wrong effect and give the correct effect.
Step 4: If one-sided error after Trial Balance → use Suspense Account.
Step 5: Pass rectifying entry in Journal Proper.
