Q.1 Answer in One Sentence

1) What is Ledger?
Ledger is the principal book of accounts that contains all accounts (Assets, Liabilities, Capital, Expenses, Incomes) in a summarized form.
2) What is ledger posting?
Ledger posting is the process of transferring entries from Journal or Subsidiary Books to the respective ledger accounts.
3) When does an account show a nil balance?
When total of debit side equals total of credit side, the account shows nil balance.
4) What is Folio?
Folio means page number; Ledger Folio (L.F.) refers to page number of ledger, Journal Folio (J.F.) refers to page number of journal.
5) Where is the statement form of ledger A/c used in actual practice?
It is used in Bank Passbooks, computerised accounting software, and modern accounting systems.
6) Why Proprietor's Capital account is a liability for the business?
Because business is a separate entity and amount invested by proprietor is considered as liability towards the owner.
7) Why does cash account never show a credit balance?
Cash balance can never be negative; business cannot pay more cash than it possesses, hence always debit balance.
8) What is 'Trial Balance'?
Trial Balance is a statement of all ledger account balances (debit and credit) prepared to check arithmetical accuracy of books.

Q.4 State True or False with reasons

1) Ledger is a book of Original Entry.
False Ledger is the book of final entry; Journal is the book of original entry.
2) The process of recording a transaction in the Journal is called Posting.
False Recording in Journal is called Journalising; transferring to Ledger is Posting.
3) A cash withdrawal from business by the trader should be credited to Drawings A/c.
False Drawings A/c is debited; Cash/Bank A/c is credited.
4) Balances of Nominal Accounts are carried forward to the next year.
False Nominal accounts are closed by transferring to Trading/P&L A/c at year end.
5) When debit side of an account is greater than the credit side, the account shows a debit balance.
True Debit balance = total debits exceed total credits.
6) The name of an account written on top of each account is called 'Head of Account'.
True Example: Cash A/c, Capital A/c are heads.
7) Agreement of Trial Balance always proves accounting accuracy.
False It proves arithmetical accuracy but not all errors (e.g., compensating errors).
8) Trial balance is based on the double entry principle that for every debit there is equal amount of corresponding credit.
True Trial balance verifies this equality.

Important: Balancing of Ledger Accounts

Personal Account balance
Debit balance = Debtor; Credit balance = Creditor
Real Account balance
Always Debit balance (assets)
Nominal Account balance
Debit balance = Expenses/Losses; Credit balance = Incomes/Gains (transferred to P&L)
c/d vs b/d
c/d = carried down (closing); b/d = brought down (opening balance next period)
