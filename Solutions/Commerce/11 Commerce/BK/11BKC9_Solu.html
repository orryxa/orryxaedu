Q.1 Answer in One Sentence

1) What is a Trading A/c?
Trading Account is prepared to ascertain Gross Profit or Gross Loss of a business by recording direct expenses and incomes.
2) What do you mean by Profit & Loss A/c?
Profit & Loss Account is prepared to ascertain Net Profit or Net Loss by recording indirect expenses and indirect incomes.
3) Why Balance Sheet is prepared?
Balance Sheet is prepared to show the financial position of the business in terms of assets and liabilities on a specific date.
4) State the meaning of Final Accounts.
Final Accounts include Trading Account, Profit & Loss Account and Balance Sheet prepared at the end of financial year.
5) What is Net Profit?
Net Profit is the excess of credit side over debit side in Profit & Loss Account after considering all indirect items.
6) What do you mean by Gross Profit?
Gross Profit is the excess of credit side over debit side in Trading Account (Sales + Closing Stock - Cost of Goods Sold).
7) State the meaning of Accrued Income.
Accrued Income is income earned but not yet received during the accounting year.
8) State the meaning of Outstanding Expenses.
Outstanding Expenses are expenses incurred but not yet paid during the accounting year.
9) What is Depreciation?
Depreciation is gradual and continuous decrease in the value of fixed assets due to use, passage of time or obsolescence.
10) What do you mean by Prepaid Expenses?
Prepaid Expenses are expenses paid in advance for the next accounting period.

Q.4 State True or False with reasons

1) In every adjustment at least there are three effects.
True Each adjustment affects Trading/P&L A/c and Balance Sheet in two places (total three effects).
2) Every item of Trial Balance has only one effect.
True Trial Balance items appear only once either in Trading/P&L A/c or Balance Sheet.
3) Income due but not received is a liability.
False Accrued income is an asset, not a liability.
4) Goodwill is not a fictitious asset.
True Goodwill is an intangible real asset, not fictitious.
5) Credit balance of Profit & Loss account shows net profit.
True Excess of credit over debit in P&L A/c is Net Profit.

Q.7 Do you agree or disagree?

1) Reserve for bad debts is created by debiting Profit and Loss Account.
Agree R.D.D. is created by debiting P&L A/c and crediting R.D.D. A/c.
2) Balance Sheet is a statement as well as an account.
Disagree Balance Sheet is only a statement, not an account.
3) Indirect Expenses are debited to Trading Account.
Disagree Indirect expenses are debited to Profit & Loss Account.
4) Bank Overdraft is treated as an Internal Liability.
Agree Bank overdraft is shown on liability side of Balance Sheet.
5) Capital is excess of Liabilities over Assets.
Disagree Capital = Assets - Liabilities.

Q.9 Calculate the following

1) Calculate Capital: Building ₹20,000, Furniture ₹15,000, Debtors ₹30,000, Investments ₹10,000, Cash at Bank ₹5,000, Plant ₹20,000.
Total Assets = 20,000+15,000+30,000+10,000+5,000+20,000 = ₹1,00,000; Capital = Assets = ₹1,00,000 (No liabilities given)
2) Machinery ₹35,500 + installation ₹4,500 purchased 1st July 2018, depreciation @7% p.a. (year ending 31st March).
Cost = ₹40,000; Period = 9 months; Depreciation = 40,000 × 7% × 9/12 = ₹2,100
3) Loan ₹3,50,000 taken on 1st Oct 2018 @12% p.a. (year ending 31st March).
Period = 6 months; Interest = 3,50,000 × 12% × 6/12 = ₹21,000
4) Annual Insurance Premium ₹8,000 paid on 1st Dec 2018 (year ending 31st March).
Period = 4 months (Dec to March); Insurance = 8,000 × 4/12 = ₹2,667; Prepaid = ₹5,333
5) Calculate Gross Profit: Purchases ₹15,500, Sales ₹30,000, Carriage Inward ₹1,200, Opening Stock ₹5,000, Purchases Returns ₹500, Closing Stock ₹18,000.
Cost of Goods Sold = 5,000 + (15,500-500) + 1,200 = 21,200; Gross Profit = 30,000 - 21,200 = ₹8,800

Important: Closing Stock Valuation

Principle: Closing Stock is valued at Cost Price or Market Price, whichever is less (Convention of Conservatism).
Example: Cost ₹46,000, Market ₹40,000 → Closing Stock = ₹40,000 (as in Illustration 3)

R.D.D. & Discount on Debtors/Creditors

R.D.D. Calculation:
Debtors after new bad debts × Rate%
If R.D.D. (old) is given, adjust in P&L A/c
Discount on Debtors:
Debtors after Bad debts & R.D.D. × Rate%
Debited to P&L A/c
Discount on Creditors:
Creditors × Rate%
Credited to P&L A/c (exception to conservatism)
Final Accounts: Trading A/c (Gross Profit/Loss) → P&L A/c (Net Profit/Loss) → Balance Sheet (Financial Position)
