Q.1 Answer in One Sentence

1) What is Double Entry System?
Double Entry System is a scientific method of recording both aspects (debit and credit) of every business transaction.
2) What is an Account?
An account is a summarized record of all transactions relating to a particular person, asset, liability, expense or income.
3) State the meaning of Single Entry System.
Single Entry System is an incomplete method of accounting that records only cash and personal accounts, not following double entry principles.
4) What is Personal Account?
Personal accounts represent persons, firms, or organizations with whom the business deals (e.g., Debtors, Creditors).
5) State the rule of Nominal Account.
The rule of Nominal Account is: Debit all expenses and losses, Credit all incomes and gains.
6) Give two examples of intangible assets.
Goodwill, Patents, Copyright, Trademark (any two).
7) State the meaning of Real Account.
Real accounts represent assets and properties owned by the business (tangible or intangible).
8) Give two examples of income and gains.
Commission Received, Interest Received, Rent Received, Discount Received (any two).
9) State the rule of Personal Account.
The rule of Personal Account is: Debit the receiver, Credit the giver.
10) How many methods of recording accounting information are there?
There are two main methods: Indian System (Conventional) and English System (Single Entry & Double Entry).

Q.4 State True or False with Reasons

1) Outstanding expense is nominal account.
False Outstanding expense is a Representative Personal Account, not nominal.
2) Capital account is a real account.
False Capital account is a Personal account (owner's account).
3) Every debit has equal and corresponding credit.
True This is the fundamental principle of Double Entry System.
4) Discount received is a nominal account.
True Discount received is an income/gain, hence nominal account.
5) Drawings account is a nominal account.
False Drawings account is a personal account (or reduction in capital).
6) Outstanding salary is a nominal account.
False Outstanding salary is a representative personal account.
7) Loan account is personal account.
True Loan account represents the lender, hence personal account.
8) Goodwill account is a real account.
True Goodwill is an intangible asset, classified under Real Account.
9) Trade discount account is a nominal account.
False Trade discount is not recorded separately in books; no separate account exists.
10) Personal transactions of proprietor are recorded in the books of account of business.
False Only business transactions are recorded; personal transactions are recorded under Drawings if related to business.
11) Motor car account is a Real Account.
True Motor Car is a tangible asset, hence Real account.
12) The rule of Nominal Account is Debit the receiver and Credit the giver.
False The rule for Nominal Account is Debit all expenses/losses, Credit all incomes/gains.
13) Bank loan account is a Nominal account.
False Bank loan account is a Personal account (liability towards bank).
14) Assets = Capital + Liabilities
True This is the fundamental accounting equation.
15) Trademark account is a personal account.
False Trademark is an intangible asset, hence Real account.

Q.7 Complete the Accounting Equation Table

1) Started business with Cash ₹ 50,000
Assets = ₹50,000 ; Liabilities = 0 ; Capital = ₹50,000
2) Purchased goods of ₹ 10,000 (on credit)
Assets +10,000 (Stock) ; Liabilities +10,000 ; Capital unchanged
3) Goods stolen ₹ 1,000
Assets -1,000 ; Capital -1,000 (Loss)
4) Sold goods for cash ₹ 5,000
Assets (Cash +5,000, Stock - cost) Net effect depends on profit; equation balances
5) Loan taken from Bank ₹ 7,000
Assets +7,000 (Cash) ; Liabilities +7,000 (Bank Loan)
Note: Accounting equation always maintains: Assets = Liabilities + Capital.
