📌 MSMED Act 2006 Classification: Manufacturing Sector: Micro (≤₹25L), Small (₹25L-₹5Cr), Medium (₹5Cr-₹10Cr) | Service Sector: Micro (≤₹10L), Small (₹10L-₹2Cr), Medium (₹2Cr-₹5Cr)

🔹 Importance of SSI: Job opportunities, Regional balance, Competitive strength, Maximum use of natural resources, Reduces migration, Support to large industries, Develops new entrepreneurs.

🔹 Advantages (11 points): Large employment, Less capital requirement, Utilization of domestic resources, Increases industrial output, Contribution to exports (40%), Equitable distribution, Earns foreign exchange, Entrepreneurship opportunities, Cost efficiency, Reduces migration, Flexibility in operation.

🔹 Challenges (9 points): Old methods of production, Inadequate finance, Raw material problem, Labour problems, Marketing problems, Transport problems, Sickness, Power shortage & irregular supply, Global competition (LPG).

⭐ Steps to Setup SSI (11 steps): 1) Decision of business area 2) Study business environment 3) Selection of product 4) Selection of place 5) Selection of technology 6) Business proposal 7) Finance 8) Registration (DIC) 9) Arrangement of resources (physical, power, staffing) 10) Production & marketing 11) Review & feedback.

📢 Latest Update (Just to Know): Cabinet approved change in classification from 'investment in plant & machinery' to 'annual turnover' - Micro (≤₹5Cr), Small (₹5Cr-₹75Cr), Medium (₹75Cr-₹250Cr).

11 Steps in Setting up Small Scale Business

1) Decision of Business Area
Analyze strengths, decide proprietorship/partnership
2) Study of Business Environment
Industrial policy, economic policy, legal environment
3) Selection of Product
Market research, competition analysis
4) Selection of Place
Nearness to market, raw material, labour, transport
5) Selection of Technology
Choose suitable technology, determine needs
6) Business Proposal
Economic, financial, technical, market appraisal
7) Finance
Arrange capital (own or borrowed) - lifeblood of enterprise
8) Registration
Apply to District Industries Centre (DIC)
9) Arrangement of Resources
Physical resources, power/water, staffing
10) Production & Marketing
Manufacture & advertise products
11) Review/Feedback
Monitor performance, plan future growth

Case Study / Situation Analysis

Case 1: Mr. Ram wants to start SSI of bicycle parts/machinery.
i) First step: Decision of business area / Selection of product. ii) Ways to raise capital: Own savings, bank loans, government schemes (REGP), KHADI & Village Industries Commission. iii) Marketing is necessary as it generates revenue; without marketing products cannot reach customers.
Case 2: Mr. Rahim worried about challenges - guide him solutions.
Solutions: Use modern technology, avail government subsidies, form cooperatives for raw material, train workers, use e-marketing, register with DIC, use renewable energy sources, focus on quality & innovation to compete globally.

Just to Know: Cabinet approves amendment to MSMED Act

📢 New Classification based on Annual Turnover (2022):
• Micro Enterprise: Turnover does not exceed ₹5 crores
• Small Enterprise: Turnover more than ₹5 crores but ≤ ₹75 crores
• Medium Enterprise: Turnover more than ₹75 crores but ≤ ₹250 crores
This change enhances ease of doing business and increases employment in MSME sector.
