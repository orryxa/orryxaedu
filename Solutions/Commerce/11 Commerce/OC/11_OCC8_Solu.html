📘 Chapter Summary: Introduction to Management

🔹 Origin: English verb 'manage' comes from Italian 'maneggiare' (to handle) and Latin 'manus' (hand) + 'agere' (to act). Management is an ancient activity essential wherever there is group activity.

📌 Definitions:
• Mary Parker Follett: "Management is an art of getting things done through others."
• Henry Fayol: "To manage is to forecast and plan, to organize, to command, to coordinate, and to control."
• F.W. Taylor: "Management is knowing exactly what is to be done and seeing that it is done in the best possible manner."
• George Terry: "Management is the process consisting of planning, organizing, actuating and controlling."
📌 Levels of Management:
• Top Level: Board of Directors, CEO, President - Framing plans & policies, long term objectives (5-20 years).
• Middle Level: Departmental Heads (Finance, Production, HR, Marketing) - Implementation of policies, link between top & lower (1-5 years).
• Lower Level: Supervisors, Foremen, Superintendents - Actual execution, directing subordinates (up to 1 year).
📌 Management as Art, Science & Profession:
• As an Art: Personal skills, creativity, regular practice, personal abilities (subjective, skill-based).
• As a Science: Systematic body of knowledge, scientific methods, cause-effect relationship, universal applicability (social science).
• As a Profession: Formal education, code of conduct, expertise, registration (emerging profession - no statutory body yet).

⭐ Conclusion: Management is a combination of Art, Science and Profession. It is not pure science like Mathematics but a social science. It is an emerging profession with growing formal education (MBA, PGDM, IIMs).

Case Study Analysis

Case 1: Pranav gives orders to subordinates, Pravin assigns work to Pratap, Pratap executes work and reports to Pravin.
i) Top: Pranav, Middle: Pravin, Lower: Pratap. ii) Pranav is Top Level Management. iii) Pratap is Lower Level - works as per instructions, directs subordinates, solves problems.
Case 2: Shankar Patil (advocate), Prashant Patil (decoration artist), Vishwas Patil (science graduate farmer exporting produce).
i) Shankar's income: Profession (advocacy). ii) Prashant's qualification: No formal degree - art/hobby based. iii) Features of profession: Formal education, code of conduct, expertise, registration.

Key Justifications

Why is management called a group oriented action?
Management involves coordination of team efforts, decisions are taken by group (shareholders/BOD) for the group (employees), and activities are done by team under manager's guidance.
Why is management considered an art?
Because it involves personal skills, creativity, regular practice, and personal abilities - just like singing or dancing, results vary from manager to manager.
Why is there no need of registration for managers?
Unlike doctors or CA's, there is no statutory regulatory body for managers; anyone can become a manager irrespective of formal education.
