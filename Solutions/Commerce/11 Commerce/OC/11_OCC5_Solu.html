📘 Chapter Summary: Public Sector Organisations

🔹 Introduction: Public Sector Organisations are owned, financed, managed and controlled by government or combination of governments. They aim at providing reliable services to customers (service motive) unlike private sector's profit motive. Industrial Policy 1956 promoted public sector.

📌 Departmental Organisation: Oldest form. Integral part of government. Financed by annual budget. Examples: Railways, Post Office, Defence, AIR. Features: Managed by govt, no separate legal entity, govt employees, delay in action, red tapism.
📌 Statutory Corporation: Created by Special Act of Parliament/State Legislature. Autonomous corporate body. Examples: RBI, LIC, ONGC. Features: Corporate body, financial autonomy, own staffing, no political interference. Merits: Quick decisions, professional management, easy to raise capital.
📌 Government Company (Companies Act 2013): Minimum 51% paid-up capital held by government. Examples: BHEL, NTPC, HMT, SAIL. Features: Separate legal entity, registered under Companies Act, own staff, public accountability (annual report to Parliament).
📌 Multinational Corporations (MNCs): Business organisation operating in multiple countries. Examples: Tata Motors, Infosys, Bata, Pepsi, Coca-Cola. Features: Huge assets, international operations, centralised control, advanced technology, professional management.

⭐ Key Comparison: Private Sector (profit motive, quick decisions) vs Public Sector (service motive, bureaucratic delays). MNCs bring foreign capital & technology but may harm domestic industries & repatriate profits.

G. Answer in one sentence

1) What is Government Company?
A company registered under Companies Act 2013 with minimum 51% paid-up share capital held by central/state government.
2) What is Departmental Organisation?
An organisation owned, managed, controlled and financed by government as an integral part of government.
3) What is Statutory Corporation?
An autonomous corporate body created by Special Act of Parliament or State Legislature with defined powers and duties.
4) What is Multinational Corporation?
A business organisation that operates in many different countries with facilities and assets in at least one country other than its home country.

Case Study Analysis

Case 1: Company with MP Govt 35%, Maharashtra Govt 35%, Govt of India 30% capital.
i) Government Company; ii) Features: Minimum 51% govt holding, separate legal entity; iii) Examples: BHEL, NTPC, SAIL
Case 2: Company with registered office in Singapore, branches in Varanasi & Hambantota providing cellular services.
i) Multinational Corporation (MNC); ii) Operates in multiple countries, centralized control from home country, international operations.
Case 3: Central government passes statute and forms autonomous organisation answerable to legislature.
i) Statutory Corporation; ii) Features: Corporate body, financial autonomy, own staffing system; iii) Example: RBI, LIC

Key Justifications

Why is there direct control of government on departmental organisation?
Departmental organisation is an integral part of government, managed by govt officials, financed by annual budget, and minister is directly responsible to Parliament.
Why is there no political interference in statutory corporation?
Statutory corporation is created by Special Act with defined powers; it has financial autonomy, own staffing, and operates independently within legal framework.
Why do MNCs help end local monopolies?
MNCs bring competition, better quality products, competitive prices, forcing local monopolies to improve efficiency and reduce prices.
