📘 Chapter Summary: Business Environment

🔹 Introduction: Business does not function in isolation. It interacts with its environment continuously. Successful business identifies, appraises and responds to opportunities and threats. (Bayard O. Wheeler: "Total of all things external to firm affecting organization & operations")

📌 Internal Environment (Controllable Factors): Value System, Vision-Mission, Management Structure, Internal Power Relations, Human Resources, Physical Assets, Marketing Resources, Financial Factors.
📌 External Environment (Uncontrollable Factors): Natural, Social, Political, Economic, Technological, Legal (also Global Environment).
📌 New Economic Policy (LPG) - 24th July 1991:
• Liberalization: Eliminating unnecessary controls - abolished industrial licensing, reduced import duties, financial reforms.
• Privatization: Reducing government involvement - disinvestment, MOU system, less political interference.
• Globalization: Integration of national economy with world economy - boundaryless world, free flow of goods, services, capital, technology.

🔹 Importance of Business Environment: Determining opportunities & threats, identifying strengths & weaknesses, continuous learning, image building, meeting competition, giving direction for growth.

🔹 Impact of LPG on Business: Increased competition, more demanding customers, rapid technological change, need for human resource development, customer-oriented market, discontinuation of budget support.

Case Study Analysis

Case 1: Court bans polythene bags; govt gives subsidy to jute industry.
i) Legal Environment & Political Environment. ii) Business must adapt to environmental concerns and government policies.
Case 2: Lok Sabha 2014 result - Sensex rose by 1000 points in a day.
i) Political Environment - political stability and confidence. ii) Other dimensions: Economic Environment (GDP, policies), Social Environment (consumer trends).
Case 3: Company developed energy efficient light tubes lasting 20x longer.
i) Technological Environment. ii) Importance: Identifies opportunities, gives competitive advantage, leads to growth & profitability.
Case 4: Government allows oil marketing under public sector to fix own prices.
i) Economic reform: Liberalization. ii) Other reforms: Privatization and Globalization.

Key Justifications

Why are external factors of business environment beyond control?
External factors like natural conditions, government policies, economic changes, and social trends are determined by outside forces and individual businesses cannot change them.
Why should business firms be aware of changes in society?
Social changes affect consumer preferences, demand patterns, and business practices, requiring firms to adapt their products and strategies accordingly.
Why does political stability build confidence among business people?
Stable political environment ensures consistent policies, reduces risk, encourages long-term investments, and signals strength to investors.
