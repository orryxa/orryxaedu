📘 Chapter Summary: Forms of Business Organisation

🔹 Private Sector Organisations: Owned & managed by private entities (individuals or group) with profit motive. Includes Sole Trading Concern, Partnership Firm, Joint Hindu Family Business, Co-operative Society, Joint Stock Company.

📌 Sole Trading Concern: Oldest form - Single owner, unlimited liability, easy formation, quick decisions, maximum secrecy, but limited capital & managerial skill.
📌 Partnership Firm (Indian Partnership Act 1932): Minimum 2, max 50 partners; unlimited joint & several liability; agreement based; types: Active, Dormant, Nominal, Secret, Minor, Partner in profits only, Sub-partner, Quasi partner.
📌 Joint Hindu Family Business: Exists only in India; managed by Karta (senior most); coparceners by birth; unlimited liability of Karta, limited for coparceners.
📌 Co-operative Society: Voluntary association, service motive, democratic (one member one vote), registration compulsory, limited liability. Types: Consumer, Producer, Marketing, Farming, Housing, Credit.
📌 Joint Stock Company (Companies Act 2013): Artificial legal person, separate legal entity, common seal, perpetual succession, limited liability, transferable shares. Private (2-200 members) vs Public (min 7, no max).

⭐ Latest Concept: One Person Company (OPC) introduced in Companies Act 2013 for solo entrepreneurs; Special Purpose Vehicle (SPV) for isolating financial risk.

Merits & Demerits at a Glance

✅ Sole Trading Merits
Easy formation, Quick decisions, Maximum secrecy, Direct motivation, Flexibility, Lower cost
⚠️ Sole Trading Demerits
Limited capital, Limited managerial skill, Unlimited liability, Lack of stability, No specialization
✅ Partnership Merits
More capital, Business secrecy, Continued existence, Flexibility, Specialization, Goodwill
⚠️ Partnership Demerits
Continuity problem, No legal status, Disputes, Non-transferability, Unlimited liability
✅ Joint Stock Company Merits
Huge capital, Professional management, Perpetual succession, Limited liability, Public confidence
⚠️ Joint Stock Company Demerits
Rigid formation, Lack of secrecy, Delay in decisions, High cost, No personal contact

H. Answer in one sentence

1) What is Sole Trading Concern?
A business owned and managed by one person who bears all risks and enjoys all profits.
2) What is Partnership Firm?
An association of two or more persons who agree to share profits of a business carried on by all or any of them acting for all.
3) What is Joint Stock Company?
A voluntary association of persons with transferable shares, separate legal status, and perpetual succession, registered under Companies Act.
4) What is Minor Partner?
A person below 18 years admitted for benefits of partnership with consent of all partners, not personally liable.

Just to Know: Latest Concepts

🌟 One Person Company (OPC): Introduced in Companies Act 2013 - allows a single person to create a corporate entity with limited liability.
🌟 Special Purpose Vehicle (SPV): A separate legal entity created for a specific objective to isolate financial risk.
🌟 Amul - Co-operative Success Story: Founded in 1946 in Anand, Gujarat by Dr. Verghese Kurien - Father of White Revolution in India.
