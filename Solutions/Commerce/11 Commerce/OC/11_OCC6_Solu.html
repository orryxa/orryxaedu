📌 SIDBI (Small Industrial Development Bank of India) - est. 2nd April 1990: Principal financial institution for MSMEs. Head office at Lucknow. Provides Direct Finance, Indirect Finance, Micro Finance. Digital initiatives: SIDBI Startup Mitra (March 17, 2016) & Udyami Mitra Portal. Subsidiaries: SVCL, CGTMSE, SMERA, ISTSL, ISARC, MUDRA.
📌 NABARD (National Bank for Agriculture & Rural Development) - est. 12th July 1982: Apex bank for agricultural finance. Provides refinancing to SCBs, RRBs, LDBs. Supervises RRBs & Cooperative Banks. Promotes Green, Blue & White Revolution.
📌 KVIC (Khadi & Village Industries Commission) - est. April 1957: Statutory body under MSME Ministry, HQ Mumbai. Promotes khadi & village industries, generates employment, organizes training, R&D, marketing support. MoU with NID for design support.
📌 Self Help Groups (SHGs): Small homogeneous group (5-20 members) for mutual help, savings, collateral-free loans. Based on "helping each other" & "unity is strength". Promotes women empowerment, entrepreneurship, democratic setup (one member one vote). NABARD initiated SHG-Bank Linkage Programme (1992).
📌 World Bank (Bretton Woods Conference 1944): HQ Washington D.C. Comprises IBRD (middle-income loans) & IDA (interest-free loans to poor countries). Goals by 2030: End extreme poverty (below $1.90/day to ≤3%) & Promote shared prosperity (bottom 40% income growth).

⭐ Key Personalities: Dr. Muhammad Yunus (Father of Microfinance) - founded Grameen Bank (Oct 1983), jointly won Nobel Peace Prize 2006. Dr. B. Sivaraman - Chairman of CRAFICARD committee for NABARD.

Key Justifications

Why is SIDBI called institute for promotion of MSMEs?
SIDBI provides direct/indirect finance, refinance, venture capital, credit guarantee, rating services, and digital platforms like Startup Mitra & Udyami Mitra exclusively for MSMEs.
Why is Mutual Trust the soul of SHG?
SHGs operate on collective responsibility, collateral-free loans based on trust, and members guarantee each other's repayment, building confidence and social capital.
Why is SHG important for women empowerment?
SHGs provide knowledge, finance, leadership opportunities, decision-making power, and socio-economic progress, enhancing women's status in family and society.
