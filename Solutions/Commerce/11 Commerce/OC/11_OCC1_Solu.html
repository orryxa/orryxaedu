📘 Chapter Summary: Business, Trade & Commerce

🔹 Human Activities: All human activities are classified into Economic Activities (for earning money) and Non-Economic Activities (for personal satisfaction without monetary gain).

Business

Production & Distribution of goods/services for profit

Profession

Expert services with specialized knowledge (fees)

Employment

Working for others (wage/salary)
📌 Business - 10 Features: Economic activity, Two parties, Profit motive, Production of goods/services, Exchange of goods/services, Dealings in goods/services, Continuity, Uncertain returns, Element of risk, Customer satisfaction.
📌 Profession - 7 Features: Aim (money), Qualification (formal education), Financial returns (fees), Capital (limited), Registration required (ICAI, Bar Council, etc.), Non-transferability, Nature of work (expert services).
📌 Employment - 7 Features: Aim (livelihood), Qualification (as per job), Monetary returns (wage/salary), No capital required, No registration, Non-transferability, Work as per contract.
📌 Business Objectives: Economic → Profit, Customers, Innovations, Optimum resource use. Social → Quality products, Avoid unfair practices, Employment, Employee welfare, Solve social problems.
📌 Classification of Business Activities:
Industry (Production - creates form utility): Primary (Agriculture, Extractive, Genetic), Secondary (Manufacturing, Construction), Tertiary (Services).
Commerce (Distribution): Trade (Internal/Home, External/Foreign - Import, Export, Entrepot) + Auxiliaries to Trade (Transport, Warehousing, Insurance, Banking, Advertising, Mercantile Agents, Communication).

⭐ Role of Profit: Returns to investors, R&D, Growth & expansion, Increases efficiency, Means of livelihood, Reward for risk, Survival.

Classification of Trade

Trade TypeSub-typeDescription
Home Trade / Internal Trade
(Within country, local currency)		Wholesale TradePurchasing in bulk from producers, selling to retailers (link between producer & retailer)
Retail TradeSelling to final consumers (link between wholesaler/ manufacturer & consumer)
Foreign Trade / External Trade
(Between countries, different currencies)		Import TradePurchasing goods and services from another country
Export TradeSelling goods and services to foreign buyers
Entrepot TradeImporting from one country and re-exporting to another (combination of import & export)

7 Auxiliaries to Trade (Aids to Trade)

#AuxiliaryFunction (Hindrance Removed)
1TransportRemoves hindrance of place / distance - mobility to men & material
2WarehousingRemoves hindrance of time - creates time utility, storage from production to sale
3InsuranceRemoves hindrance of risk - compensates for loss due to fire, theft, accident
4Banking / FinanceRemoves hindrance of finance - provides loans, credit, overdraft, transaction facilities
5AdvertisingRemoves hindrance of information - informs customers about products (indoor/outdoor)
6Mercantile AgentsRemoves hindrance of contact - bridges gap between buyer & seller (brokers, estate agents)
7CommunicationRemoves hindrance of communication - exchange of information (phone, email, postal)

H. Answer in one sentence

1) What is an Economic Activity?
Any activity conducted for earning money or livelihood is called economic activity.
2) What is Non-economic Activity?
Human activities performed for personal satisfaction without monetary expectations are non-economic activities.
3) What is Wholesale Trade?
Wholesale trade involves purchasing goods in bulk from producers and selling in smaller quantities to retailers.
4) What is Entrepot Trade?
Entrepot trade involves importing goods from one country and re-exporting them to another country.
5) What is Primary Industry?
Primary industry is concerned with nature and includes agriculture, extractive and genetic industries.
