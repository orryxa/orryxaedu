Q.5 Answer in Brief (Detailed Explanations)

1) State four types of deposits.

  • Fixed Deposit (FD): Fixed amount for fixed period, higher interest, withdrawal not allowed before maturity (penalty). FDR issued.
  • Recurring Deposit (RD): Monthly fixed deposit; suitable for salaried persons; lump sum with interest on maturity.
  • Saving Account: For individuals; low interest; encourages savings; passbook facility; withdrawal limits.
  • Current Account: For businessmen; no interest; unlimited transactions; overdraft facility; cheque book.

2) State four modes of transport.

  • Road Transport: Uses roads; door-to-door service; flexible; suitable for short distances (buses, trucks, cars).
  • Rail Transport: Uses railway tracks; carries heavy/bulky goods over long distances; less affected by weather.
  • Air Transport: Fastest mode; uses aircraft; expensive; suitable for valuable/perishable goods and emergencies.
  • Water Transport: Uses oceans/rivers; cheapest for bulky goods; slow; promotes international trade (ships, boats).

3) State four life insurance policies.

  • Whole Life Policy: Insured for whole life; premium low; sum payable to nominee on death of insured.
  • Endowment Policy: For specific period; sum assured + bonus paid on maturity or on death (whichever earlier).
  • Term Insurance Policy: Pure protection for specific term; lowest premium; benefit paid only on death during term.
  • Money-back Policy: Periodic survival benefits (every 5 years) + full sum assured on maturity or death.

4) State any four features of business services.

  • Intangibility: Services cannot be seen, touched, or felt — only experienced (e.g., banking, insurance).
  • Inseparability: Service and service provider cannot be separated; production & consumption happen simultaneously.
  • Inconsistency (Heterogeneity): Quality may vary each time, even from same provider (e.g., restaurant experience).
  • Perishability: Services cannot be stored for future use; unused capacity is lost forever (e.g., empty flight seat).

5) State money remittance services of postal department.

  • eMO (Electronic Money Order): Electronic version of money order; money delivered at home of payee.
  • iMO (Instant Money Order): Web-based instant transfer (₹1,000 to ₹50,000) through designated iMO post offices.
  • International Money Transfer: Through Western Union partnership; instant remittance from 195+ countries to India.
Q.7 Attempt the Following (Detailed Answers)

1) Explain money remittance services of post department.

The Department of Posts provides three main money remittance services:

  • eMO (Electronic Money Order): An electronic version of traditional money order. The sender fills an eMO form, pays amount + commission. Money is delivered at the payee's doorstep by postman. Maximum limit ₹10,000.
  • iMO (Instant Money Order): Web-based instant transfer service available at iMO-enabled post offices. Transfer amount from ₹1,000 to ₹50,000. Money is available for payout within minutes at destination post office.
  • International Money Transfer: India Post has partnered with Western Union. Allows instant receipt of personal remittances from 195+ countries. Beneficiary receives cash at any designated post office.

2) Explain marine insurance policies.

  • Voyage Policy: Covers risk for a specific voyage irrespective of time. Risk begins when ship starts voyage and ends at destination.
  • Time Policy: Covers risk for a definite period (max 1 year). May have continuation clause until voyage completes.
  • Mixed Policy: Combination of voyage and time policy — covers specific voyage for specified time period.
  • Valued Policy: Agreed value of goods at time of policy; claim settled at agreed value — useful when market value is difficult to assess.
  • Fleet Policy: Several ships of same owner insured under one policy.
  • Port Risk Policy: Covers vessel while anchored at port for specific period.

3) Explain types of warehouses.

  • Private Warehouse: Owned by large manufacturers/merchants for own storage. High capital required.
  • Public Warehouse: Licensed by govt; provides storage to general public for a fee. Located near transport hubs.
  • Bonded Warehouse: Licensed to store imported goods until customs duty paid. Under customs control.
  • Duty Paid Warehouse: Storage after customs duty payment. Useful for re-export.
  • Government Warehouse: Owned by central/state govts (CWC, FCI). Nominal charges for farmers/traders.
  • Co-operative Warehouse: Owned by co-op societies; economical rates for members.
  • Cold Storage Warehouse: Low temperature storage for perishable commodities (fruits, vegetables, dairy).

4) Explain utility functions of banks.

  • Issue of Drafts & Cheques: Bank issues demand drafts (for account & non-account holders) and cheques (only for account holders).
  • Locker Facility: Safe custody of valuables, documents, gold ornaments for customers.
  • Project Reports: Banks prepare feasibility studies and project reports to help clients obtain funds.
  • Gift Cheques & Gold Coins: Banks issue gift cheques and sell gold coins to customers.
  • Underwriting Services: Bank guarantees to take up unsubscribed portion of securities issued by companies.
  • Gold Related Services: Buying/selling gold ornaments, gold funds, Gold ETF advisory.

5) Explain disadvantages of air transport.

  • Very Expensive: High freight charges — not affordable for ordinary goods or small businesses.
  • Affected by Weather: Flights delayed or cancelled due to fog, storms, heavy rain, snow.
  • Not Suitable for Short Distances: Cost-effective only for long distances; for short distances, road/rail is better.
  • Huge Investment: Requires large capital for airports, runways, air traffic control, aircraft maintenance.
  • International Restrictions: Some nations restrict overflight; requires diplomatic permissions.
  • No Door-to-Door Service: Goods must be transported to/from airport by road.
Q.8 Answer the Following (Long Answer Type with Details)

1) What is insurance? Explain principles of insurance.

Meaning: Insurance is a contract between insurer and insured where insurer agrees to compensate insured against specified losses in exchange for premium. It is a risk management tool.

7 Principles of Insurance:

  • Utmost Good Faith: Both parties must disclose all material facts truthfully. Concealment voids the contract.
  • Insurable Interest: Insured must have financial interest in subject matter — would suffer loss if damaged. Life: at contract time; Fire/Marine: at contract & loss time.
  • Indemnity: Insurer compensates actual loss to restore pre-loss financial position. Not applicable to life insurance.
  • Subrogation: After compensation, rights of damaged property transfer to insurer. Prevents double profit.
  • Contribution: If multiple policies exist, each insurer pays proportionate share of loss.
  • Mitigation of Loss: Insured must take reasonable steps to minimize loss after an event.
  • Causa-Proxima: Nearest/dominant cause of loss determines liability — not remote causes.

2) Define bank. Explain different types of banks.

Definition: As per Indian Banking Regulation Act, 1949 — "Banking means accepting for the purpose of lending or investment of deposits of money from public, repayable on demand."

  • Central Bank (RBI): Apex institution; issues currency; banker to govt & banks; frames monetary policy.
  • Commercial Bank: Accepts deposits & lends to public — SBI, HDFC, ICICI.
  • Co-operative Bank: Provides credit to farmers & small scale units at village/district/state levels.
  • Development Bank: Medium & long term finance for industrial expansion — IFCI, SIDBI, MSFC.
  • Exchange Bank: Facilitates foreign exchange & international trade — LC, forex remittance.
  • Regional Rural Bank (RRB): Serves rural areas; sponsored by public sector banks (est. 1975).
  • Savings Bank: Encourages small savings — Postal Savings Bank.
  • Investment Bank: Advisory, M&A, underwriting — not for general public.
  • Specialised Banks: EXIM (trade), SIDBI (MSME), NABARD (agri/rural).

3) What is warehouse? Explain its different functions.

Meaning: Warehouse is an establishment for storage/accumulation of goods from production till consumption.

  • Storage: Basic function — surplus goods stored till demand arises.
  • Price Stabilization: Releases goods when supply is low, absorbs when supply is high — stabilizes prices.
  • Risk Bearing: Protects goods from theft, fire, deterioration; warehouse keeper is liable.
  • Financing: Loans can be raised against warehouse receipts.
  • Grading & Packing: Provides grading, packing, processing as per owner's instructions.
  • Transportation: Collects goods from production point and delivers to customers.
  • Time & Place Utility: Preserves goods till demanded (time) and makes available where required (place).
  • Processing: Some commodities need processing — paddy polishing, fruit ripening undertaken by warehouses.

4) What is Services? Explain in detail different business services.

Definition (Kotler): "A service is an act that one party can offer that is essentially intangible and does not result in ownership."

Five major business services:

  • Banking: Financial institution accepting deposits & lending — provides current/saving/FD accounts, loans, e-banking (ATM, RTGS, NEFT, IMPS).
  • Insurance: Risk management contract — Life, Marine, Fire insurance based on principles of utmost good faith, insurable interest, indemnity, etc.
  • Transport: Movement of goods/people — Road (door-to-door), Rail (bulk), Air (fastest), Water (cheapest), Pipeline, Ropeway.
  • Warehousing: Storage of goods — creates time utility; functions: storage, price stabilization, financing, processing.
  • Communication: Exchange of information — Postal (mail, eMO, iMO, ePost) and Modern (courier, internet, email).

5) What is communication? Explain in detail various types of communication.

Meaning: Communication is the art of exchanging ideas, facts, information from one person/entity to another using some medium.

I. Postal Services (India Post):

  • Mail Services: Inland letter, envelope, parcel, book post.
  • Specialised Postal: Business Post, Logistics Post, Bill Mail Service.
  • Money Remittance: eMO, iMO, International Money Transfer (Western Union).
  • Retail Services: Retail Post (bill collection), e-Post (electronic to hard copy delivery).

II. Modern Means:

  • Courier Service: Private companies (DHL, DTDC, FedEx) — speed, tracking, signature.
  • Internet: Global network of interconnected computers using TCP/IP — WWW, email, file sharing.
  • Email: Electronic mail exchange via store-and-forward servers.

6) What is road transport? Explain its advantages and disadvantages.

Meaning: Transport using roads — vehicles like buses, trucks, cars, bullock carts, cycles.

Advantages:

  • Cheap mode for short distances.
  • Perishable goods transported faster over short distances.
  • Flexible — loading/unloading at any destination.
  • Provides door-to-door service.
  • Serves as feeder to other modes (rail, air, water).
  • Connects remote areas not served by other transport.

Disadvantages:

  • Limited carrying capacity — not economical for long distance.
  • Heavy/bulky goods transport involves high cost.
  • Affected by weather (floods, rain, landslides).
  • High risk of road accidents.
  • Causes pollution (gases, noise).
